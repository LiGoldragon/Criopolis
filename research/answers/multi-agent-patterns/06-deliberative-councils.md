# Deliberative Councils

## What Counts as a Council Pattern

A deliberative council is not just multiple agents. It has stable seats, distinct disciplines, a proposition or case, visible disagreement, and a final editorial or judicial act. Criopolis has all five ingredients: stable council seats named Satya, Viveka, Dharma, Prayoga, and Rasa; propositions framed by mayor; seat-specific output contracts; bead notes as visible replies; and mayor synthesis as final stance (`agents/mayor/prompt.template.md:73-130`, `agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`, `agents/viveka/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/dharma/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/prayoga/prompt.template.md:71-85`, `agents/rasa/prompt.template.md:71-92`).

## Practical Precedent: Expert Panels

Claude Code plugin workflows are practical expert panels. The feature-dev workflow asks separate code-explorer agents to inspect different aspects, separate architect agents to propose different approaches, and separate reviewer agents to inspect simplicity, correctness, and conventions (`/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/feature-dev/commands/feature-dev.md:36-53`, `/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/feature-dev/commands/feature-dev.md:73-82`, `/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/feature-dev/commands/feature-dev.md:101-109`). The code-review workflow uses multiple specialized reviewers and validation subagents, then filters unvalidated issues (`/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/code-review/commands/code-review.md:30-57`). These are council-like in role specialization and synthesis, but they are task panels, not a persistent civic high council.

## Debate and Dialectic

Multiagent debate is the clearest LLM-agent precedent for deliberation. Du et al. propose multiple model instances that debate their answers and reasoning over rounds to reach a common final answer (Du et al., "Improving Factuality and Reasoning in Language Models through Multiagent Debate", 2023-05-23, https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14325). CAMEL provides a role-playing implementation where assistant and user agents alternate messages and may include a critic (`/git/github.com/camel-ai/camel/camel/societies/role_playing.py:36-90`, `/git/github.com/camel-ai/camel/camel/societies/role_playing.py:631-710`). AutoGen group chat provides a runtime substrate for repeated turns, termination, and speaker selection (`/git/github.com/microsoft/autogen/python/packages/autogen-agentchat/src/autogen_agentchat/teams/_group_chat/_base_group_chat_manager.py:86-193`).

Criopolis is related but different. The council seats are not multiple copies debating the same answer; they are different evaluative disciplines with separate required ledgers and failure modes (`agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`, `agents/viveka/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/dharma/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/prayoga/prompt.template.md:71-85`, `agents/rasa/prompt.template.md:71-92`).

## Devil's Advocate and Adversarial Collaboration

Devil's advocate and dialectical inquiry are organizational decision techniques that assign dissenting or opposing analysis to reduce premature agreement. A representative management-science lineage is Mason's work on dialectical inquiry (Mason, "A Dialectical Approach to Strategic Planning", Management Science, 1969, https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.15.8.B403) and Schwenk/Valacich's later comparison of devil's advocacy and dialectical inquiry for individuals versus groups (Schwenk and Valacich, "Effects of Devil's Advocacy and Dialectical Inquiry on Individuals Versus Groups", Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes, 1994, https://experts.azregents.edu/en/publications/effects-of-devils-advocacy-and-dialectical-inquiry-on-individuals/). Adversarial collaboration is a related scientific practice in which disagreeing parties jointly design tests or analyses; Kahneman and Klein's bounded-rationality collaboration is a known example (Kahneman and Klein, "Conditions for Intuitive Expertise: A Failure to Disagree", American Psychologist, 2009-09, https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19739881/).

Criopolis's Satya and Viveka seats are especially close to adversarial roles. Satya requires source bones, falsification ledger, claim type, and vote (`agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`). Viveka requires a terminology audit and checks for superimposition and term smuggling (`agents/viveka/prompt.template.md:71-92`). The important design choice is that disagreement is not treated as a problem to erase; mayor is instructed to push back on real disagreement and iterate until disagreement is real rather than semantic (`agents/mayor/prompt.template.md:121-127`).

## Red-Team / Blue-Team

Red-team/blue-team patterns split attack and defense roles. The surveyed codebase evidence is strongest in Claude Code's code-review workflow: bug agents inspect for significant defects, then validation subagents check whether those defects are real before comments are posted (`/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/code-review/commands/code-review.md:30-77`). This maps to a red-team generation plus verifier filter. Criopolis can reproduce this pattern by slinging a code proposal to Satya for factual/code evidence, Prayoga for load/application, and a separate validation researcher for contested issue claims (`agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`, `agents/prayoga/prompt.template.md:71-85`, `agents/researcher/prompt.template.md:27-44`).

## Talmudic, Scholastic, Aristotelian, and Vedic Analogies

The bead asks specifically about Aristotelian, Vedic, Talmudic, and scholastic dialectic adapted to agents. In the surveyed implementation source, I found no codebase that implements those traditions directly as a multi-agent architecture. What Criopolis does have is a named high council with Sanskrit-derived seat names and discipline descriptions: Satya as truth/correspondence, Viveka as discrimination/cutting, Dharma as right-order/who-is-missing, Prayoga as application/under-load, and Rasa as felt-disclosure/form-savor (`agents/mayor/prompt.template.md:83-89`). The prompt contracts make these more than labels: each seat has its own output format and checks (`agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`, `agents/viveka/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/dharma/prompt.template.md:71-92`, `agents/prayoga/prompt.template.md:71-85`, `agents/rasa/prompt.template.md:71-92`).

The closest academic analogies are debate, dialectical inquiry, adversarial collaboration, and society-of-mind. The closest implementation analogies are AutoGen group chat, CAMEL role playing, Claude Code expert panels, and Anthropic Research's lead/subagent architecture (`/git/github.com/microsoft/autogen/python/packages/autogen-agentchat/src/autogen_agentchat/teams/_group_chat/_base_group_chat_manager.py:86-193`, `/git/github.com/camel-ai/camel/camel/societies/role_playing.py:36-90`, `/git/github.com/anthropics/claude-code/plugins/feature-dev/commands/feature-dev.md:36-109`, Anthropic, "How we built our multi-agent research system", 2025-06-13, https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/multi-agent-research-system). The exact Criopolis shape was not found in surveyed source.

## Council Failure Modes

Council architectures can fail by verbosity, fake consensus, ungrounded objections, and role drift. Anthropic reports early multi-agent research failures such as spawning too many subagents, searching endlessly for nonexistent sources, and excessive updates (Anthropic, "How we built our multi-agent research system", 2025-06-13, https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/multi-agent-research-system). Claude Code docs warn that many subagents returning detailed results can consume significant main-conversation context (Anthropic, "Create custom subagents", accessed 2026-05-04, https://code.claude.com/docs/en/subagents). Criopolis mitigates these with bead durability, majordomo noise absorption, researcher citation discipline, and seat-specific output contracts (`agents/majordomo/prompt.template.md:31-50`, `agents/researcher/prompt.template.md:27-44`, `agents/satya/prompt.template.md:71-101`, `agents/rasa/prompt.template.md:71-92`).

The remaining weakness is synthesis overload. Mayor still has to read and judge many seat outputs unless majordomo's first-pass synthesis is strong and structured (`agents/mayor/prompt.template.md:121-130`, `agents/majordomo/prompt.template.md:96-108`).
