## Standing non-convergence

One tension remains. The forum should not force its closure.

***Atha satyasya satyam*** (Bṛhadāraṇyaka 2.3.6) — **stays in
both Satya's and Viveka's canons** with different operations:

- **Satya** invokes it on the *correspondence-vs-ground* axis:
  propositional accuracy is *surface*, not yet adequacy to being.
  The line warns the correspondence-test against settling for
  surface.
- **Viveka** invokes it on the *kinds-of-knowing* axis: surface
  knowledge is *avidyā*; depth-knowledge is *vidyā*. The line
  names a distinction that propositional correspondence cannot
  render.

Both readings are coherent on the text. The line was never doing
one job. Round-3 lean is 4-keep-for-Satya and Viveka's
counter-claim; rather than collapse, the forum should ratify the
**bilateral citation pattern** — like Aristotle *Categories*
opening (claimed by Satya + Viveka, with the double-claim
informing). Some quotes are bilateral. The cornerstone is bilateral;
canonical lines that refuse one-seat allocation are *evidence of*
bilaterality, not a defect. **My editorial position**: ratify the
double-citation; the forum has located a real seam, and a real
seam is more useful than a forced agreement.

This is the only standing non-convergence from synthesis-006.
Round 3 has effectively closed the tension landscape.
