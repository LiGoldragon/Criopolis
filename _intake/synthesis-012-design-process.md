# Synthesis-012 — Council design-process (round 8)

*Mayor synthesis after round-8 council deliberation on how the council
produces one ratified design proposal rather than five competing ones.
All five seats voted yes-with-closures on a single converged shape; this
synthesis names the ratified process, the closures each seat made
load-bearing, and the open downstream questions.*

## Position the council ratified

A **single-draft hybrid** design-process: design packet opens the
process; one topic-affinity author writes the first draft; reviewers
pass the same packet sequentially with force-tagged deltas; mayor (or a
seat-native final writer for narrow artifacts) integrates with a
disposition log; a kind-conditional savor-pass fires on re-read-class
artifacts; council ratifies with object-on-classification rights.

Vote: **5/5 yes-with-closures**.

- Satya: yes / rule (`_intake/replies/round-8/satya.md`).
- Viveka: yes; no to five competing drafts; no to mayor-only finals on
  authority-changing designs (`_intake/replies/round-8/viveka.md`).
- Dharma: yes, conditional on the closures (concerns-appendix
  mandatory; diff record; author-selection reason; bound-party test;
  seat objection rights). Without closures, defer
  (`_intake/replies/round-8/dharma.md`).
- Prayoga: yes; no to five competing drafts for design artifacts;
  defer exact route matrix until two P1 designs have run
  (`_intake/replies/round-8/prayoga.md`).
- Rasa: yes / rule, with kind-conditional savor-pass and three
  pass-closures (`_intake/replies/round-8/rasa.md`).

## The ratified shape

### 1. Design packet opens the process

Mayor (or majordomo, when it exists) posts a packet naming:

- **artifact** path or deliverable;
- **force class** — `editorial` / `design-substantive` / `keel-or-live-structural` (Viveka's taxonomy);
- **audience class** — `re-read` / `first-reading` (Rasa's addition);
- **first author** and selection reason;
- **reviewer route** — subset or all-five, in order, with reason per seat;
- **final writer** — mayor by default, seat-native when justified;
- **substantive boundary** — what would re-open council or Li;
- **acceptance check** — dry-run, example, transcript, sample bead;
- **mirror path / closure** — `_intake/replies/<topic>/` and
  `_intake/<area>/` paths set before bead closes;
- **deletion or review condition** — when the artifact is reconsidered.

The packet is shorter than the artifact unless the artifact is
production-affecting or keel-force.

### 2. Topic-affinity author selection

The first author is selected by the artifact's central failure mode,
not by rotation. Rotation is fair to people; affinity is fair to the
work.

| central failure mode | first author |
|---|---|
| truth-claim, evidence-bound, success-criteria | Satya |
| term/scope/role-boundary, category overgrowth | Viveka |
| bound-party, authority-line, stakeholder burden | Dharma |
| operational, under-load, runbook, script | Prayoga |
| form, report, prompt-readability, re-read-curve | Rasa |
| orchestration, city-process, composes-with-synthesis | Mayor |

**Anti-ossification rule** (Dharma): when multiple seats have affinity,
rotate within the affinity-set across designs of the same class. The
selection reason is logged in the packet so the rotation is visible.

If no seat has clear affinity, mayor drafts and routes review.

### 3. Routed sequential review with force-tagged deltas

Reviewers do not submit competing drafts. Each comment carries two
tags.

**Force tag** (Prayoga's four-label scheme):

- `must-fix / blocker` — accepting the draft as written changes
  authority, cost, failure mode, or decision test in a way the
  process has not licensed;
- `should-fix / required` — improves the artifact but does not block;
- `editorial / suggestion` — wording or form, no behavior change;
- `defer` — belongs to another seat, Li, or a later bead.

**Substantive/editorial tag** — explicit on every comment, tested by
the operational cut below.

**Reviewer order** is per-design, governed by the design's
highest-risk unknown. Default chains:

- Terms/scope unstable → Viveka first.
- Bound parties / authority shifts → Dharma early.
- Source/path/factual claims load-bearing → Satya before final.
- Will be run, installed, scripted → Prayoga before final.
- Re-read-class form / inhabited prompt → Rasa late, after the parts
  have been assembled.

Mayor records the chosen order in the packet.

### 4. Mayor as default final writer

Mayor is the default final writer for governance, process, and
multi-discipline artifacts. A seat is final writer when the artifact is
seat-native — Rasa for a rapporteur template, Viveka for a terminology
table, Prayoga for an operational checklist.

The final writer:

- integrates the artifact into one usable object;
- records a **disposition log / concerns-appendix** for every blocker
  and required comment — *accepted / modified / declined-with-reason
  / escalated*;
- may make editorial calls (wording, ordering, examples,
  condensation, choosing among non-blocking alternatives);
- **may not silently override a substantive blocker**. Allowed paths:
  revise to satisfy, record a reasoned disagreement and route to
  all-five ratification, or escalate to Li.

The disposition log is **load-bearing**, not decoration (Dharma's
position): without it, the final pass is the parallel-then-merge
process with one less round of competing drafts and the same opacity.

### 5. Kind-conditional savor-pass (Rasa's addition)

Fires only on **re-read-class** artifacts — designs the inheritor will
read at session start, repeat-load, or governance-comparison: role
prompts, council-process documents, the Li-rapporteur template, keel
rules.

Withheld on **first-reading-class** artifacts (one-shot setup scripts,
single-action runbooks). The withdrawal is logged as
*silence-as-finding* — a recorded bound, not an absence.

Operation when fired:

1. First reading at the moment of mayor's final pass.
2. Real interval — at least one council day — attending to other
   things; the pass-runner does not stay on the artifact.
3. Public context noted — what attention happened during the interval
   (a sibling artifact, a piece of canon, a council bead). Named, not
   detailed. This is what saves the pass from private taste.
4. Second reading.
5. **Curve report** — *thickens / thins / holds*. Thins → blocker.
6. Result feeds the ratification gate as a normal comment.

Pass-runner is per-artifact, not per-seat:

- Form-class-disclosure-curve → Rasa.
- Term-stability across re-readings → Viveka.
- Runtime-curve under repeat-load → Prayoga.

### 6. Ratification gate

Each routed seat records on the final draft:

- `ratify` — accept;
- `object-on-incorporation` — "my must-fix was dropped without
  disposition" (triggers re-review);
- `object-on-classification` — "a for-record concern should have been
  must-fix" (triggers council vote, 3-of-5 overrides mayor);
- `object-on-substantive-classification` — "mayor classified a
  substantive change as editorial" (triggers council vote on
  classification).

Non-routed seats retain the standing right to flag the artifact in a
following round (see §Standing right below); they do not vote at the
ratification gate of a subset-routed design.

## Substantive vs editorial — three convergent tests

The substantive/editorial cut is operational, not adjudicated by mayor
discretion. Three tests, mutually reinforcing — a change is
substantive if **any** test triggers:

- **Authority test** (Satya / Viveka): the change alters *scope, force,
  trigger, read-write surface, artifact required, live-host effect,
  reviewer test, failure mode, exception path, deletion-or-review
  condition, or the meaning of a ratified term*.
- **Bound-party test** (Dharma, *zhèng míng* applied): the change
  adds, removes, or reframes a party not in the room.
- **Form-curve test** (Rasa, bounded to re-read class): the change
  thins the form's second-reading curve, even when the authority test
  and bound-party test pass.

A change is editorial only if it preserves all three tests. Mayor
classifies on first pass; any seat may object-on-classification at the
ratification gate; council adjudicates 3-of-5.

## Subset vs all-five — three force classes (Viveka)

| force class | examples | required route |
|---|---|---|
| `editorial` | typos, cross-links, wording-preserving-force, template layout within ratified fields | mayor / final writer authority; log source if useful |
| `design-substantive` | new role prompt, staging script, rapporteur template, non-keel operating rule | affinity drafter + targeted reviewers + all-seat **notice**; all-five review when the role graduates to live |
| `keel / live-structural` | keel rule, host subsidy verbs, live deployment power, workspace boundary, new council authority | all five seats or explicit Li decision; final artifact carries closures |

**Notice ≠ review.** Non-routed seats retain notice (the packet is
visible in the bead store) and the standing right of proposal. A
subset means "not on the critical review path," not "not allowed to
care." Any routed reviewer can force all-five via a substantive blocker.

## Standing right of proposal (Li's rule, ratified)

Any seat may flag a future-round issue at any round. Mayor logs the
flag with:

- proposition (one-sentence claim);
- claim-type — `substantive-now` / `substantive-backlog` /
  `editorial-mayor-can-handle`;
- **bound party that triggered the flag** (Dharma's amendment) — if no
  party can be named, flag is registered as `forum-procedural`,
  distinguished but valid;
- failure mode predicted;
- proposed action — full council / subset design / Li decision /
  delete;
- mayor's recommended timing — `next` / `later` / `blocked` / `delete`.

Mayor brings the flag forward in the next-round candidate list. The
right is *to be entered and answered*, not to automatically consume the
next round.

## Required closures

- **Route closure** — packet logs author, reviewers, final writer,
  force class, audience class before drafting begins.
- **Mirror closure** — final artifact and seat replies land under
  `_intake/...` before bead closes.
- **Override closure** — every blocker and required comment has
  disposition logged: accepted / modified / declined-with-reason /
  escalated.
- **Subset closure** — subset routes record why the subset is
  sufficient and notice non-routed seats.
- **Install closure** — artifacts that grant new authority are drafted,
  not installed; Li approval or council ratification recorded before
  live use.
- **Review/deletion closure** — every recurring rule, prompt, or
  process names when it is reviewed or retired.
- **Self-application closure** — future process-design rounds use
  *this* process recursively. Round 8 was a one-off use of the prior
  parallel-deliberation method as meta-deliberation; the next process
  revision goes through the new process.

## What this answers from prior synthesis

Synthesis-011 (round-7 ratified architecture: hybrid-with-subsidy +
roles cohort + keel contract) flagged Viveka's diairesis —
substantive vs editorial keel changes — as round-8 work. Synthesis-012
answers it: the **three-test operational cut** (authority + bound-party
+ form-curve) is the substantive/editorial joint, not mayor's
adjudication alone. The disposition log + object-on-classification
gate enforce it procedurally.

The "every-change-voted" anti-pattern from `keel.md` is honored: the
process-revision itself is council-required, but per-design editorial
work falls under mayor authority bounded by the disposition log and the
seat objection rights.

The lightest-first injunction from synthesis-011 is honored: most
designs route through a subset; only role-graduation, keel rules, live
authority, and council-binding decisions trigger all-five.

## What this changes for mayor's operating discipline

- Mayor opens a design packet before slinging.
- Mayor selects first author by affinity and logs the reason.
- Mayor records reviewer order in the packet.
- Mayor produces the disposition log on the final pass.
- Mayor classifies substantive vs editorial on first pass; seats can
  object-on-classification.
- Mayor logs every standing-right flag with the seven fields above and
  brings them to the next-round candidate list.
- Round 8 was the last use of the prior parallel-deliberation method
  for design-output questions. Future design-process revisions use
  this process.
