  Western correspondence.

  makes the Aristotle citation do twice the work.

  (Dharma): the criterion-of-truth regress. Western-skeptical

  the *logos*…" — pre-Socratic anti-room-agreement anchor,
  *library-local*.
- **Claim-type line before vote** (Prayoga): tag each
  proposition `fact / definition / frame / rule / recommendation
  / experiment` so the next-action distinguishes by type.

**TENSIONS** (Li to resolve, or defer to next round).

1. ***atha satyasya satyam* — does it belong to Satya or Viveka?**
   - Satya's elaboration uses it as the Indic-depth anchor.
   - Viveka claims it back: "that distinguishes deeper truth from
     surface truth — that is *my* operation, not correspondence."
   - Dharma + Rasa keep it for Satya as load-bearing.
   - **Vote pattern: 1 cut (Viveka), 2 keep (Dharma + Rasa),
     1 ambiguous (Prayoga: "drift toward Viveka's station,
     soften").**
   - Substantively: the line names a depth-of-truth distinction,
     which is *kinds-of-knowing* work — Viveka's. But Satya's
     elaboration treats it as the warning that propositional
     correspondence is *surface*, not *ground* — which is
     Satya's domain ("don't settle for surface-correspondence").
     Both readings are coherent. **Tension is real.**

2. **Muṇḍaka 3.1.6 *satyam eva jayate* — keep solo line, expand
   to couplet, or cut "test of time" gloss?**
   - Satya proposes expanding to *satyena labhyas tapasā* +
     *satyam eva jayate nānṛtaṃ* (full couplet 3.1.5–6).
   - Viveka warns Satya's gloss "truth as what survives every
     test, including the test of time" smuggles a coherence /
     time-survival criterion into a correspondence seat.
   - Resolution candidate: keep the couplet (Satya's expansion
     is right) but cut the time-survival gloss (Viveka's
     correction is right).

3. **Falsification ledger — name Popper or use neutral term?**
   - Viveka: silent borrow; either name Popper as ancestor or
     use "refutation-conditions ledger."
   - Others didn't address.
   - Resolution candidate: name Popper. Cheap; protects against
     Viveka's class of correction.

**QUOTES** (Satya's own refinement). Proposes **four**, not three.

1. Aristotle *Metaphysics* Γ.7, 1011b26 — keep verbatim.
2. **Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad 3.1.5–6** (couplet, replaces single line).
3. Bṛhadāraṇyaka 2.3.6 *atha satyasya satyam* — keep verbatim
   *(contested; see Tension #1)*.
4. **Patañjali *Yoga Sūtra* II.36** *satya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ
   kriyā-phalāśrayatvam* — add. Truth as *yama*, not just
   propositional property.
