# bead-jswt — Vedic works on balanced thinking / acting / resting

## Walk of what's already in `library/`

The library is genuinely strong on the Vedic side. The triple-balance
theme is **already well-served** for these threads:

- **Patañjali / eight limbs / sthira-sukha-āsanam (YS II.46)** —
  Bryant (FSG 2009, epub), Arya + Vyāsa-bhāṣya (Himalayan 1986, pdf),
  Woods (Harvard Oriental 1914, pdf), Veda Bharati Vol I (catalog).
  Three independent commentary traditions; well-covered.
- **Tridoṣa / dincharyā / Āyurvedic balance** — Caraka (Sharma),
  Suśruta (Bhishagratna), Vāgbhaṭa (Murthy). The full bṛhat-trayī.
- **Three guṇas / Sāṃkhya** — Īśvarakṛṣṇa with Tattva-Kaumudī
  (Virūpākṣānanda), Larson *Classical Sāṃkhya* + *Encyclopedia
  Vol IV*, Burley *Classical Sāṃkhya and Yoga*, Dasgupta Vols I–II.
- **Kaṭha Upaniṣad chariot, Upaniṣadic substrate** — Olivelle's
  *Early Upaniṣads* (Sanskrit/English), Deussen's *Sixty*,
  Radhakrishnan, Hume, Gambhirananda + Śaṅkara on the Eight.
  Full coverage.
- **Yoga Upaniṣads** — Ayyangar (Adyar) is present.
- **Haṭha corpus** — Svātmārāma's *Pradīpikā* (Muktibodhānanda),
  Gheraṇḍa + Śiva Saṃhitās + Khecarīvidyā (all Mallinson).
  Mallinson & Singleton *Roots of Yoga* (the source-book).
- **Subtle anatomy** — Avalon's *Serpent Power*.
- **Yoga–Āyurveda unity** — Frawley, Feuerstein, Zysk, Wujastyk.

**Discovered gaps (catalog vs. disk):** `en/bepin-behari/`,
`en/raj-bali-pandey/`, `en/sharma-dash/`, and `sa/yoga/` +
`sa/ayurveda/` are present as **empty directories** — the catalog
promises books that have not actually been fetched. Worth a
librarian sweep.

**The real gap** for *balance-of-thinking-acting-resting* is the
**Bhagavad Gītā commentary tradition** (only Van Buitenen's
Mahābhārata-embedded scholarly text is present — the karma/jñāna/
dhyāna triple is under-served), and the entire **Advaita-Vedānta
abhyāsa-vairāgya-viveka** stream.

## 8 priority-adds

### 1. Ādi Śaṅkara — *Vivekacūḍāmaṇi* (Crest-Jewel of Discrimination)
- **Edition:** Swami Madhavananda trans. (Advaita Ashrama, 1921;
  Sanskrit + English + notes; many reprints). Anna's MD5
  `4ccb1c709f13d8bb9c7ca5276b69a298` (1995 14th impression) or
  `116a00928fec70aced237399fe2ed2ce` (1978).
- **Why:** Locus classicus of the **viveka–vairāgya–abhyāsa**
  Vedantic triple the bead names. 580 verses unpacking
  discrimination, dispassion, and steady practice as the threefold
  ladder. Currently no work in the library names this triple.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** yes — the verse text is short, edition includes
  Devanagari; add to `sa/sankara/`.

### 2. *Yoga Vāsiṣṭha* — Swami Venkatesananda, *The Supreme Yoga*
- **Edition:** Venkatesananda (Chiltern Yoga Trust, 1976; Motilal
  reprint). Anna's `fb10c40be004476771fa88b1c5a1c337`.
- **Why:** The longest sustained classical text on **resting-as-
  witness amid action** — Vasiṣṭha teaches Rāma how to act without
  being moved. The single best primary source on the
  acting-while-resting pole that the Gītā only sketches.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** the 32,000-verse Bombay/Nirnaya-Sagar critical
  edition is a heavy haul; defer unless asked.

### 3. *Bhagavad Gītā with Śaṅkara's Bhāṣya* — Swami Gambhirananda
- **Edition:** Gambhirananda (Advaita Ashrama, 1984; Sanskrit +
  English). Anna's hash for the Madhusūdana + Gambhirananda combined
  edition is `7061be8d22c2322a2b0933900080630c`.
- **Why:** The classical Advaita commentary on the **karma /
  jñāna / dhyāna** triple. Library has only Van Buitenen's
  Mahābhārata-embedded text — no commentary tradition on the central
  text of the triple-yoga theme. Śaṅkara's bhāṣya is the foundation
  every later Gītā reading flows through.
- **Library status:** partial (text present; classical commentary
  absent). **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** included in the edition; sufficient.

### 4. Sri Aurobindo — *Essays on the Gītā*
- **Edition:** SABCL Vol 13 / *Complete Works of Sri Aurobindo*
  Vol 19 (Sri Aurobindo Ashram). Anna's
  `2c14bbf3224aeae32de9526eb901ea4d`.
- **Why:** The major modern philosophical synthesis of
  karma/jñāna/bhakti as the **integral movement** — the most
  developed 20th-century treatment of the balance-among-paths
  question. Pairs with Gītā text + Śaṅkara above.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** not relevant — original English composition.

### 5. *Aṣṭāvakra Gītā* — Swami Nityaswarupananda
- **Edition:** Nityaswarupananda trans. (Advaita Ashrama, 1953;
  reprints). Anna's `dd547f7ece2ac90b91d8aaf9cc34a1b4` (2023 epub).
- **Why:** ~300 verses — the most concentrated articulation of
  **pure resting-awareness as the Self**. The ground state at the
  end of the resting pole; necessary counter-weight to the
  action-heavy Gītā corpus.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** yes, short text; add to `sa/astavakra/`.

### 6. Vidyāraṇya — *Pañcadaśī* — Swami Swahananda
- **Edition:** Swahananda trans. (Sri Ramakrishna Math, Chennai,
  1967). Anna's `f17b2e0c7efa9b16a6b87e0fae5d1f84`.
- **Why:** 14th-c. Vedanta synthesis treating the three states —
  **waking, dreaming, deep sleep** — as evidence for the witness
  that is constant across them. Direct mapping onto
  thinking/acting/resting as states the witness traverses; the most
  systematic post-Śaṅkara treatment of this exact structure.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** yes; add to `sa/vidyaranya/`.

### 7. B.K.S. Iyengar — *Light on the Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali*
- **Edition:** Iyengar (HarperCollins / Aquarian Press, 1993; 2002
  reprint). Anna's `58fab65246a0e164711e7122b504b4d5`.
- **Why:** Library already has Bryant, Arya/Vyāsa, Woods — three
  *scholarly* commentaries. Iyengar adds the **practitioner-asana-
  tradition** voice on the very verse the bead cites
  (II.46 *sthira-sukham āsanam*) and II.47–48 (the resolution of
  effort and effortlessness). Different discriminatory value than
  yet-another-scholarly-edition.
- **Library status:** partial (sūtras well-covered; this voice
  missing). Add.
- **Sanskrit:** redundant — covered by existing editions.

### 8. Swami Vivekananda — *Karma-Yoga / Rāja-Yoga / Jñāna-Yoga / Bhakti-Yoga*
- **Edition:** standalone Advaita Ashrama volumes, OR the
  *Complete Works of Swami Vivekananda* (9 vols, Advaita Ashrama).
  The Vibhatsu 2024 omnibus epub
  (`61c2a4e760268ac916b997e3686a6a2d`) is the cleanest single-file
  capture of the four yogas.
- **Why:** The clearest modern articulation that yoga is **not one
  path but a quaternary of paths to balance among** —
  action/knowledge/meditation/devotion. Names the balance-of-paths
  question in its modern form; the source every later "integral"
  reading borrows from.
- **Library status:** absent. **Priority-add.**
- **Sanskrit:** not relevant — modern English lectures.

## Lower-priority companion (not in the 8)

**Eknath Easwaran — *The Bhagavad Gita / The Upanishads / The
Dhammapada*** (Nilgiri Press, 1985–87). Practitioner-tradition
counterparts to the scholarly Olivelle / Van Buitenen / Bryant
editions already present. Useful for *lived-balance* readings (the
Eight-Point Program is itself a thinking/acting/resting program),
but the texts are already covered scholarly-side. Add only if the
forum needs the pastoral register.

## Recommended order of acquisition

If the librarian fetches in priority order: **1 (Vivekacūḍāmaṇi),
3 (Gītā + Śaṅkara), 5 (Aṣṭāvakra), 6 (Pañcadaśī)** are the four
indispensable additions — they fill the entire viveka/vairāgya/
abhyāsa axis the library currently lacks. **2 (Yoga Vāsiṣṭha)** and
**4 (Aurobindo)** are the next tier. **7 (Iyengar)** and
**8 (Vivekananda)** are practitioner-side voices that round out
the corpus.
