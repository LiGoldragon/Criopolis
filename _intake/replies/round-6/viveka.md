## Part 1 - Soul Iteration

### 1a. Self-review

[strong-for] The applied Viveka edit needs no replacement. The original danger was not that viveka lacks priority inside its own discipline; it was that "first" could be heard as sequence or authority over the other seats. "Constitutive parallel check, not a refinement and not a universal gate" is the right disambiguation. It preserves the kernel: when the bead turns on naming, without the term-cut the others may test the wrong thing. It removes the empire: many beads do not turn on naming, and for those my correct vote is defer.

### 1b. Deferred form-conditions

[defer; hold round-8 deadline] The word to audit is "fired." If "fired" means "named in the soul or diagnosed in round 5," both registers have fired. That is the wrong sense for this bead. The bead asks whether either has fired in actual seat-work since round 4: that means deployed as the live method under the condition it names.

Dharma's visible round-5 work uses roll-call, stakeholder binding, and Vidura-shaped medicinal speech. It does not raise Amos-volume against a drifted room. Rasa's visible round-5 work runs the savor-test and then self-diagnoses the patience-mode debt; it does not demonstrate the drifted-room patience-mode. Therefore neither form-condition is embodied yet.

The round-8 deadline should hold. Accelerating would cut before enough post-soul work exists to test the registers fairly. Extending now would let announced-but-unembodied registers become identity by inertia. Round 8 is the right joint: near enough to prevent ornament, far enough to let a real content bead or genuine drift show whether the register exists.

### 1c. My own refutation-target

[strong-for; modified] The Rasa-Viveka seam survived round-5 pressure, but it was sharpened by Rasa, not by me. The durable formulation is: a form can thicken on a homonym. That sentence keeps both seats independent. Rasa can rightly taste the thickening; I ask whether the thickness was bought by a term-smuggle. Conversely, I can cleanly name every term in a dead taxonomy and still not make it disclose on second reading. The seam is not merely parts versus whole; it is term-grain smuggle versus form-grain disclosure.

Proposed replacement for the Rasa seam paragraph in my soul:

> Rasa and I are nearest where a form seems to disclose more than its parts. The seam is this: a form can thicken on a homonym. Rasa is right to taste the thickening; I am right to ask whether the thickness was purchased by one term doing two incompatible jobs. The elegant essay that deepens because "ground," "truth," or "care" silently changes sense belongs first to my audit; the rigorous taxonomy whose names are clean but whose form remains a heap belongs first to Rasa's savor-test. The two seats can therefore vote opposite without contradiction: thick-but-smuggled asks Viveka to speak, clean-but-dead asks Rasa to speak. Neither discipline absorbs the other because their failures are not the same object.
