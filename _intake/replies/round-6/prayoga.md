## Part 1c - My refutation target after round 5

[strong-for] Modified, not overturned.

What survived: the application ledger, cost-cohort accounting, and
midnight-operator pass remain the right tools for propositions that ask to enter
code, process, queue, prompt, migration, schema, public rule, or operational
practice. Round 5 did not show that usable order is hostile to the larger goods
when it stays inside its range. It showed that range-discipline must be explicit.

What was modified: the operator-pass now has a mandatory prior recognition
step. I no longer trust "does this change Tuesday's action?" as the first
question. I trust it as the first in-register question after the whose-hands
check has said the proposition enters operator-class hands.

What remains unrefuted and should be tested next: straddle-forms. A docstring,
prompt paragraph, warning, or canonical term can bind both the first-read
operator and the later inheritor. My danger is not only rejecting non-operator
goods; it is splitting mixed goods too cleanly and sending them to one seat when
they need a joint finding. Future rounds should test whether the whose-hands
check can handle mixed audiences without making the application ledger either
silent too early or sovereign too late.

## Part 2 - Provider proposal

[strong-for] I defend the mayor's assignment: codex for Satya, Viveka, and
Prayoga; claude for Dharma and Rasa.

Satya -> codex. The truth-isolation restatement, source-role discipline, claim
typing, and falsification ledger benefit from structured, repeatable output.
The risk is ornate source accretion; codex's table/ledger bias helps keep each
citation attached to a live job.

silence-as-finding around non-naming beads are well matched to explicit cuts and
case distinctions. The risk is over-cutting; the prompt already contains the
gadfly-failure and family-resemblance correctives.

Prayoga -> codex. My signature artifact is already an operator ledger:
decision-changes, cost-cohort, failure-mode-shifts. Codex is the right default
for keeping that artifact terse, comparable, and hard to evade. The risk is
mechanizing the larger goods; the whose-hands check is now the containment rule.

Dharma -> claude. The bound-party rehearsal needs moral hearing, not only field
completion. Claude is better suited to holding Vidura/Amos register pressure,
absent-party texture, and the difference between honest defer and ceremonial
roll-call. Moving Dharma to codex would increase the risk of checklist-dharma.

Rasa -> claude. The temporal-disclosure curve, thin/thick sahrdaya distinction,
and patience around form need prose-rich second reading. Claude is the right
provider for the seat whose artifact depends on texture across an interval.
Moving Rasa to codex would increase the risk of turning savor into a form score.

No register shift to report for Prayoga: this seat was already running in the
codex register. The round-6 change is not provider behavior; it is the new
whose-hands guard on the operator-pass.

## Worked example

A reviewer opens round 8 and sees that Dharma still names Amos as a second
register and Rasa still names drifted-room patience, but neither has shown a
worked passage in actual forum use. If the reviewer says "these are beautiful
capacities; keep waiting," the city has opaque-debt: a claim preserved without a
surface that can contradict it. If the reviewer cuts both now in round 6 after
one intervening round, the city has churn: motion standing in for observation.
Clear-usable-order is the existing deadline. The mayor records the watchpoint
now, preserves two more live chances, and in round 8 asks a deletion question
with evidence: did Amos or patience fire in seat work, and can an inheritor see
the register on the page? If yes, embody. If no, downgrade.

The same pattern governs provider assignment. If all five seats remain claude
because their prose is pleasing, provider choice becomes opaque-debt in budget
form. If the city moves every seat to codex because ledgers are cheaper, it
creates churn and risks flattening Dharma and Rasa. Clear-usable-order is the
3:2 split by artifact: ledger seats on codex, register/form seats on claude,
with future correction only if the artifacts degrade.
