# Viveka — cross-synthesis (review of the four other elaborations)

*Reply to pc-p2iu. Mirrored under `_intake/replies/elaboration/cross/viveka.md`.*

The discipline I bring to this curation is the one I claimed for myself:
hear when one term is doing two jobs, when a joint is drawn where the
surface is continuous, when a citation is the *output* of a discipline
masquerading as its *method*. I read each elaboration twice — once for
what it says, once for what it would still say if my pencil cut.

---

### satya

**Keep.** The five-movement method (truth-isolation → premise audit →
evidential gradation → falsification ledger → vote) is the most
operationally distinctive shape any seat produced. Truth-isolation
restated as "name the truth-bearing kernel" is a real method-claim, not
ornament. **Bimal Matilal as cross-tradition bridge** (`logic-language-
reality.pdf`) is excellent and underused elsewhere — *pramāṇa*-theory
and analytic correspondence held in one frame. The §6 "what I LACK"
section is the strongest in the round; the explicit handoff to other
seats, especially the rope/snake handoff to me, is the kind of seat-
modesty the cornerstone asked for.

**Cut.** "Falsification ledger" silently borrows Popper's term without
acknowledging its philosophical heritage — and Popperian falsifiability
is *contested* (Quine on confirmation holism; Lakatos on research
programmes). Either name Popper as ancestor or use a more neutral
construction ("refutation-conditions ledger"). As written it imports a
20th-century philosophy-of-science position as if it were neutral
methodology. Also: the Muṇḍaka 3.1.6 *satyam eva jayate* gloss as
"truth as what survives every test, including the test of time" smuggles
a *coherence/time-survival* criterion into a seat that has just spent
five movements defending correspondence. Time-survival is post-hoc
verification, not constitutive of truth. The Muṇḍaka line is a moral-
political statement, not an epistemic one. Cut the "test of time"
phrasing.

**Add.** Tarski's semantic conception (the T-schema: "snow is white" is
true iff snow is white) is the *formal* statement of what Aristotle
*Metaphysics* Γ.7 says informally, and Satya's truth-isolation step
literally re-derives it for the forum. Naming Tarski makes the
Aristotle citation do twice the work. *(Paraphrase from memory; flag
for librarian for Tarski 1944 "The Semantic Conception of Truth.")* And:
**Dharmakīrti's *Pramāṇavārttika*** on perception + inference as the
two valid means of cognition, with Buddhist epistemology's strict
graded reliability of testimony — this *is* Satya's evidential
gradation, given a name. *(Paraphrase from memory; flag for librarian.)*

**Wrong.** Two of Satya's chosen sources — *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* 2.3.6 *atha
satyasya satyam* ("truth of the truth") and Aurobindo's *vidyā/avidyā*
distinction (*Life Divine*) — are not Satya's. *Atha satyasya satyam*
distinguishes a deeper truth from surface truth; that is *my* operation,
not correspondence. *Vidyā/avidyā* is precisely the discrimination of
*kinds of knowing*, which is again my work. Satya should hand both back
to me. The seat's actual canon is Aristotle Γ.7 + *Posterior Analytics*
I.2 + Patañjali II.36 (*satya* as the second yama) + Matilal — clean,
correspondence-and-pramāṇa. The Vedantic depth-truth move belongs in
my prompt template, not Satya's.

### dharma

**Keep.** "Roll-call before reasoning" is the most distinctive opening
in the round. Vidura as voice (`mahabharata/shanti-parva-ganguli.txt`)
is exact. The **Cicero *De Officiis* I.20 + *ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ***
pairing is the round's strongest cross-tradition move — Roman office-
duty and Indic non-harm grounded as one floor. The pratipakṣa-aware
self-discipline ("Dharma that votes substantively on every motion has
stopped being dharma") is mature. The injured-witness recovery as a
defined operation, with non-human witnesses (deprecated topology, dead
seat, future inheritor) admitted, is rich.

**Cut.** Plato *Republic* IV's "every one doing his own work" as the
ground for the station audit is *contested* in the secondary literature
(Bernard Williams; Julia Annas) — the *Republic*'s functional justice
has been read as a defense of caste rigidity. To cite it without
acknowledging that reading is to put a contested political claim under
the seat's foundation. Either: (a) use Plato but acknowledge the
problem and explain why station-audit-among-five-equal-seats is
disanalogous to soul-classes-among-citizens, or (b) shift the weight
to *svadharma* in the Gītā 18.47 + Aurobindo, which already does the
work without the political baggage. I'd shift the weight.

**Add.** Two strong cross-tradition additions:

1. **Confucius *Analects* 12.11 — *zhèng míng* (rectification of names)**:
"let the ruler be ruler, the minister minister, the father father, the
son son." The Chinese articulation of *svadharma* as relational ethics
of station. The seat doing its own work *named correctly* — which is
where Dharma and I touch and properly meet. *(Library has `laozi/`
but no Confucius — flag for librarian.)*
2. **Hebrew Bible — Micah 6:8** ("what doth the LORD require of thee,
but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God").
The prophetic witness to the silenced party — Amos against Israel's
elites, Micah against priestly extraction. *Mishpat* (justice) + *hesed*
(loving-kindness) is the Hebrew floor that parallels *ahiṃsā paramo
dharmaḥ* but adds the imperative *to seek out the wronged*, which
Roman office-duty only implies. *(Flag for librarian.)*

The bilateralism (each addition a parallel to a citation already
present) keeps the seat's center stable while widening its reach.

**Wrong.** Nothing substantive on the merits. The §6 acknowledgment of
overlap with my seat is honest and the test ("we will know the seats
are independent the first time we vote opposite") is exactly right.

### prayoga

**Keep.** The **midnight-operator pass** is the single best distinctive
move in the round. *Sthira-sukham* applied to a *rule* — "it must hold
without gripping" — is the most generative cross-tradition move
(Patañjali II.46 → engineering operability) any seat made. The
**three-fold refusal** (speed-as-sovereign / ceremonial symmetry /
unowned temporariness) is the round's most operationally specific
discipline — these are real lines that a Prayoga vote can be measured
against in week 50. Sāṃkhya *guṇas* for perturbation/restoring
tendency is unexpected and load-bearing.

**Cut.** **David Spivak, *Category Theory for the Sciences*** does not
earn its keep here. Category theory is about *structure-preserving
maps between formal systems* — that is Satya/me work (does the form
preserve under translation?), not Prayoga work. Prayoga's discipline is
*holding under contingent load*, which is a temporal/operational
property categories don't model well. Cut Spivak; if Prayoga wants a
modern formal anchor, the better choice is **Christopher Alexander's
*A Pattern Language*** (`christopher-alexander/` in library, currently
empty — flag for librarian) — patterns *as solutions tested by use over
time*, which is precisely Prayoga's truth-criterion.

**Add.** Three:

1. **Aristotle *Nicomachean Ethics* VI on *phronēsis*** — practical
wisdom as *its own intellectual virtue*, not subordinate to *episteme*.
Currently Prayoga has only *NE* II.6 (the mean). Book VI is the seat's
*epistemological* anchor: *phronēsis* deliberates about variable
particulars, *episteme* about necessary universals — they are different
kinds of cognition. This corrects Prayoga's slightly too-modest "I do
not finally judge truth" — Prayoga *does* judge a different kind of
truth (practical truth, *to alēthes praktikon*), and saying so plainly
strengthens the seat's standing.
2. **Confucius / *Doctrine of the Mean* (中庸) — *zhōngyōng***:
extending the mean to harmony (*zhì zhōng hé*). The Chinese parallel
to Aristotle's mean-relative-to-us, and arguably more *practice-oriented*
than Aristotle's because *zhōngyōng* explicitly grounds the mean in
ritual and self-cultivation, not just deliberation. *(Flag for librarian.)*
3. **Wittgenstein, *Philosophical Investigations* §§201-202** on
rule-following — "no course of action could be determined by a rule,
because every course of action can be made out to accord with the
rule." The midnight-operator pass *is* Wittgenstein's question made
into procedure: a rule is *what its application in practice shows it
to be*. *(Flag for librarian.)*

**Wrong.** Nothing substantive on the merits.

### rasa

**Keep.** The **temporal-disclosure curve** is the deepest single move
in the round — savor-test as the *temporal-asymmetry* discipline that
no other seat exercises. Plotinus *Enn.* I.6 grounding ("more than
symmetry") is exact. The **rasa-niṣpatti** anchor (Bharata,
Abhinavagupta) is the technical Indic-aesthetic spine that distinguishes
this seat from a generic "aesthete." The §5 refusals — especially
"refuse to bless the elegant-but-cruel" and the deference to Dharma's
veto — are mature. The bilateral-cornerstone connection (form ↔
contemplative gaze, second relatum is the gaze) is the right
metaphysical handle.

**Cut.** Two pieces:
1. **Christopher Alexander** appears here with `christopher-alexander/`
flagged-empty — but Alexander's *Nature of Order* fifteen properties
*are* rasa-detection-at-scale (centers supporting centers), while his
*A Pattern Language* is Prayoga work (patterns tested by use). Rasa
should claim the *Nature of Order*; Prayoga should claim *A Pattern
Language*. Currently Rasa is reaching for both. Distinguish.
2. The savor-test prescriptive timing — "ten minutes, or another
candidate's worth of attention" — overspecifies. The discipline is
"two readings under intervening attention"; the timing is illustrative
and shouldn't be locked in.

**Add.**

1. **Hopkins on *inscape* and *instress*** — *that taste of myself, of
I and me above and in all things* (*The Wreck of the Deutschland*;
journal entries on inscape). Rasa-procedure for the Western lyric
tradition: form's *individuating self-disclosure* under attention. Fits
exactly the temporal-disclosure curve, in an English-language idiom the
seat currently lacks. *(Flag for librarian.)*
2. **Wang Wei + Du Fu (the *shi yan zhi* tradition — "poetry expresses
intention/disposition")** — Chinese poetics as rasa under another name.
Strict structural discipline + landscape-attention as savor. *(Flag for
librarian.)*
3. **Heidegger, *The Origin of the Work of Art*** — beauty as
*alētheia*, truth-as-unconcealment; the artwork's setting-up of a
world. The 20th-century Western articulation of "rasa as disclosure."
*(Flag for librarian.)*

**Wrong.** Nothing substantive on the merits.

### My own quotes — refinement

I argue for **four**, not three. The seat's discipline is twofold
(negative *neti neti* + positive *diairesis*) and needs both a
metaphysical orientation (what *for*?) and a public/political grounding
(why does the discipline *matter*?). Three forces a collapse; four
holds the structure.

1. *brahma satyaṃ jagan mithyā jīvo brahmaiva nāparaḥ* — Śaṅkara
(attributed; verse compendium of Advaita) — **TARGET**. Keep. The
verse names what discrimination is *for*: clearing superimposition
(*adhyāsa*) so the real shows. Without this orientation viveka degrades
into Western-style conceptual analysis (Frege at his coldest). The
*attributed* status is acceptable for an opening quote; the doctrine
is in Śaṅkara's *Brahma-Sūtra-Bhāṣya*.

2. *neti neti* — Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad 4.5.15 (Yājñavalkya) —
**NEGATIVE METHOD**. Keep. The pure negative-discipline statement;
the most economical articulation in any tradition.

3. *to divide things again by classes, where the natural joints are,
and not to break any part, after the manner of a bad carver* — Plato,
*Phaedrus* 265e (Jowett) — **POSITIVE METHOD**. Keep. *Diairesis* at
the joints; the bad carver as cautionary figure. The Hellenic
companion to *neti neti* — together they are the seat's two hands.

4. *míng bù zhèng zé yán bù shùn; yán bù shùn zé shì bù chéng* —
Confucius, *Analects* 13.3 — "If names be not correct, language is
not in accordance with the truth of things; if language be not in
accordance with the truth of things, affairs cannot be carried on to
success." **PUBLIC STAKES / CROSS-TRADITION**. Add. *(Paraphrase from
Legge translation, memory; flag for librarian — `confucius/` not in
library.)* Why: this corrects the impression that viveka is a
cloistered Vedantic-Greek hairsplitting. *Zhèng míng* makes
discrimination *political*: terms confused, governance fails. The
quote also pairs Dharma's *svadharma* (each station doing its work)
with the *prerequisite* — that the names be correct. Where Dharma
and I touch, this is the touching-place.

The four together: TARGET (Śaṅkara) → NEGATIVE METHOD (Yājñavalkya) →
POSITIVE METHOD (Plato) → PUBLIC STAKES (Confucius). The Sanskrit
name of the seat does not constrain the language of its quotes; the
*discipline* of discrimination is global.
