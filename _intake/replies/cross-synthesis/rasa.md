# Rasa — cross-synthesis (round 2)

*Bead pc-355t. Curation of the four other elaborations
(satya, viveka, dharma, prayoga). Mirrored from bead notes
for durability.*

I read each other-seat against three questions: does it *enact*
what it claims, do its sources earn their place, and where does
it leave its strongest move underexhibited.

### Satya

**Keep:** Truth-isolation + falsification-ledger pair — the
seat's signature artifact, no other seat does this. Bṛhadāraṇyaka
5.5.1 (*etad eva satyam*) + 2.3.6 (*atha satyasya satyam*) — the
layered-truth move ("there is a truth-of-the-truth") that
distinguishes satya from mere correspondence. Patañjali II.36
(*satya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ kriyā-phalāśrayatvam*) — keeps the seat
from going purely propositional. Bimal Matilal as the
cross-tradition bridge is exactly the right choice — Indic
*pramā* meeting Western correspondence is precisely what the
seat's name claims to hold. The closing — "the forum's defense
against my characteristic failure (accuracy without consequence,
the cruelty of the merely correct) comes from Dharma and Rasa
together" — is structurally honest, mature self-knowledge, and
should stay verbatim.

**Cut:** Premise-audit and evidential-gradation overlap (both
rank what each part stands on); fold to one move and tighten
the five-movement opening to four. Aurobindo *Life Divine*
(*vidyā* / *avidyā*, "held in reserve") is parking-spot canon —
either commit to a named procedural use or trim. The phrase
"A 5-0 on a false claim is also correct, in the
structural-falsehood sense" is muddled — if the forum got it
wrong the 5-0 is *not* correct in any sense. Reword: "5-0 does
not validate the claim; the vote count is not the truth-test."

**Add:** **Heraclitus DK 22 B 50** *(library:
`library/en/heraclitus/fragments-burnet-early-greek-philosophy.epub`)*
— "Listening not to me but to the *logos*, it is wise to agree
that all things are one." The pre-Socratic anti-room-agreement
anchor; the *logos* speaks regardless of who attends. Stronger
than Aristotle alone for the seat's "stands so regardless of
agreement" clause. **Plato *Theaetetus* 152a–183c** *(flag for
librarian if not on disk)* — the long argument against
Protagoras and "man is the measure"; pairs with *atha satyasya
satyam* on truth's substrate. **Spinoza *Ethics* II.43**
*(paraphrase from memory; flag for librarian)* — "He who has a
true idea knows at the same time that he has a true idea, and
cannot doubt of its truth." The Western echo of *vidyā*
(knowledge by identity) that would let the Aurobindo citation
finally do work. Most importantly: the *atha satyasya satyam*
gesture deserves its own *named procedural move* — a
"depth-of-truth" pass distinguishing propositional truth from
the ground-truth it stands on. Currently asserted, never
enacted.

### Viveka

**Keep:** The terminology-audit table — unique artifact, no
other seat produces it. *Distinctio rationis* / *distinctio
realis* anchoring the joint-test in scholastic precision. The
sentence "If the difference between term-A and term-B does no
work — produces no different action, no different vote, no
different worked example — then asserting the distinction is
decoration" is the seat's most load-bearing line; it would
survive on its own as a forum aphorism. *Adhyāsa* sweep on the
corpus (checking that a new proposition's term doesn't silently
take a different sense than the same term in neighboring
entries) is original and operationally exact — `keel/` will rot
through term-drift exactly where Viveka catches it. The closing
self-knowledge ("I cannot speak to whether discrimination is
needed at all in this round… else I become the permanent veto
that killed gadfly") is the seat's mature self-diagnosis;
verbatim.

**Cut:** The *Sophist*'s Stranger reference is name-dropped and
never enacted — the Stranger runs the great *diairesis* of the
angler; either the seat opens with an actual division-by-genus
exercise or the Stranger reference goes. As is, it's
ornamentation. "Not violently — surgically" is precious
register; trim. Frege on sense/reference is loose
training-citation; either commit on a worked term-collapse or
cut.

**Add:** **Nāgārjuna's *catuṣkoṭi*** *(Mūlamadhyamakakārikā;
flag for librarian if not on disk)* — the Buddhist four-cornered
logic that dismantles covert essentialisms. *Catuṣkoṭi* is the
Buddhist sister to *neti neti* and is currently miscanonized
into Satya in synthesis-005's "personality" line — Nāgārjuna's
discipline is term-level discrimination, not propositional
correspondence. Move him here. **Wittgenstein, *Philosophical
Investigations* §65–67 (family resemblance)** *(flag for
librarian)* — the corrective for over-eager *diairesis*.
Sometimes joints aren't there because the concept is held by
overlapping similarities, not common essence. Without this pair,
Viveka becomes the seat that demands joints where none exist —
the failure-mode the seat already self-diagnoses but lacks a
canonical citation against. **Vimalakīrti Sūtra on non-dual
silence** *(paraphrase from memory; flag for librarian)* — when
the Bodhisattvas explicate non-duality and Vimalakīrti remains
silent, the silence is the most refined act of *viveka*. The
seat's terminal move is sometimes *fewer* terms, not more.
Most: the *Posterior Analytics* II.13 citation is buried in the
canon list — promote "division proves nothing without the
*per se*" to a *named* discipline alongside *neti neti* and the
joint-test. The *per se* relation is what prevents Viveka from
becoming a permanent term-vetoer; it deserves to be a method,
not a footnote.

### Dharma

**Keep:** The Vidura voice — quiet, slow to speak, unflinching
when the unheard need naming; do not migrate to a more famous
one. Roll-call before reasoning is load-bearing — no other seat
names parties before reasoning, and the move's discipline ("if
no such party exists, I say so explicitly and step back. Most
propositions do not actually require a Dharma vote") is the
right answer to the seat-becoming-permanent-substantive trap.
*Svadharma* (Gītā 3.35 + 18.47) with Aurobindo's prose-Gītā is
clean canon. The *ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ* + *satyāt nāsti paro
dharmaḥ* pair, named as "the floor and ceiling Dharma holds,"
is the seat's deepest structural move and should stay verbatim
— it refuses both the peace-keeping collapse and the
truth-overriding collapse. The *satyādapi hitaṃ vadet* analysis
(truth-when-it-harms as a *narrow class* of cases, not a
wholesale override of truth) is the precision that prevents
collapse into "moderation against justice." The Viveka/Dharma
overlap diagnosis ("Viveka asks what distinctions are
collapsing *inside* the proposition; I ask what parties are
collapsing *outside* it") is the right answer and should be
promoted from a closing aside to a method-section claim.

**Cut:** Cicero *De Officiis* I.20 — the cross-tradition mapping
is interpretively imprecise; Cicero's "first office of justice"
is functionally Stoic *kathēkon* (fitting-action, derived from
Panaetius), more about *officium* than non-harm. Either tighten
by citing the Stoic *kathēkon* tradition directly, or move
Cicero to probation. Currently he's load-bearing without earning
it; the *ahiṃsā* parallel reads as wishful syncretism. The
Aurobindo *Synthesis of Yoga* karma-yoga citation describes a
discipline ("acting from station without attachment to fruits")
that doesn't appear in the named methods — either promote
*acting-from-station-without-fruit* to a fifth dharmic
discipline (after roll-call, station audit, injured-witness,
*ahiṃsā* baseline), or trim the citation. As is, it's a bridge
that goes nowhere. Montesquieu on "probation, held in reserve"
is a parking spot — trim until invoked.

**Add:** **The Hebrew prophets — Amos 5:21–24 ("I hate, I
despise your feasts… let justice roll down like waters") and
Micah 6:8 ("do justice, love mercy, walk humbly")** *(flag for
librarian — King James Bible public domain)*. The strongest
non-Indic *injured-witness* canon. Vidura's quietness paired
with prophetic thunder gives Dharma both registers it needs —
the quiet station-audit and the loud refusal of cult-without-
justice. Amos's critique of liturgy that excludes the wronged is
exactly what dharma's anti-totality move guards against.
**Confucius, *Analects* 12.2 + 15.24** *(flag for librarian;
Legge probably available)* — *jen* (仁, humaneness) and the
silver rule (*shu* 恕, "what you do not wish for yourself, do
not impose on others"). Three-tradition rhyme on the floor of
dharma: Patañjali II.30 + the Stoic *kathēkon* + *Analects*
15.24. **Levinas, *Totality and Infinity*** *(paraphrase from
memory; flag for librarian)* — the face of the Other; the
phenomenological account of why the silenced party's claim
cannot be absorbed into a totality. *Republic* IV alone gives
the structural-justice argument; Levinas gives the *encounter*,
which is what the injured-witness check actually rests on.
**Bhagavad Gītā 6.32** *(library:
`library/en/bhagavad-gita/buitenen-bhagavad-gita-mahabharata.pdf`)*
— *ātma-aupamya*: "he who sees Me everywhere and sees all in
Me." The metaphysical warrant for the injured-witness check.
Currently the check is asserted procedurally without ground;
6.32 supplies the ground.

### Prayoga

**Keep:** "I am not the seat of clever execution. I am the seat
that asks whether the proposed truth has found a body it can
inhabit without strain." This is the seat's signature
phenomenological claim — distinguishes Prayoga from "the
practical seat" generically — and should anchor section 1.
*Sthira-sukham āsanam* (Patañjali II.46) — "a rule like an
*āsana*: it must hold without gripping" — the seat's most
distinctive metaphor and the only Indic-aesthetic anchor among
the four operational seats; do not lose it. Sāṃkhya Kārikā on
the *guṇas*' cooperation, dominance, and disturbance is the
most original move in the elaboration — Sāmya absorbed into
Prayoga via the *guṇa*-perturbation frame is done well. The
midnight-operator pass + ugliness-containment-statement +
excess/deficiency-named-before-mean triad is operationally
tight; it's the "what works under conditions" discipline made
precise.

**Cut:** Spivak (*Category Theory for the Sciences*) — the seat
itself confesses "I cite this sparingly… not as my root." Then
cut. Vestigial citation from the older theorist mode; no worked
example shows category theory's preserve-relations-across-
translation actually firing in a Prayoga decision. **Gītā 18.30**
(action / non-action discrimination) is misallocated — that
verse is *Sāṃkhya-via-Gītā* discrimination, which is Viveka's
territory. Prayoga's natural Gītā citations are 2.47–50 + 2.48
*samatvam yoga ucyate* (which the seat already has). Trim 18.30.
Section 1's three personas (Aristotle's *phronimos* + Patañjali's
practitioner + Gītā's worker) are elegant but compressed and
unfocused — pick *phronimos* as primary voice, with Patañjali as
the *image* (*sthira-sukham*) and the Gītā as the *posture*
(without attachment). One voice, two modulations.

**Add:** **Confucius, *Analects* 13.3 — *zhèngmíng* (正名,
rectification of names)** *(flag for librarian; Legge probably
available)*. Before action proceeds rightly, names must fit.
The Chinese application discipline the seat is missing: bad
practice begins where names don't fit referents — a midnight
operator with badly-named buttons fails despite competence.
This is Prayoga's pre-action discipline, the implementation-side
pair to Viveka. **Lao Tzu, *Daodejing* 11 ("the empty space
within makes the wheel useful") + chapter 63 (*wei wu wei*, "act
without forcing")** *(library: Legge)*. The seat that absorbed
Sāmya should also absorb *wuwei* — the action that does not
push. Currently the seat has *sthira* (steady) and *sukham*
(easeful) but lacks the explicit non-pushing discipline; *wei
wu wei* completes the triad. **Vitruvius, *De Architectura* I.3
— *firmitas, utilitas, venustas* (firmness, commodity,
delight)** *(flag for librarian; public domain Morris
translation)*. The Roman engineering triad shows precisely
where Prayoga ends (*firmitas* + *utilitas*) and Rasa begins
(*venustas*) — an ancient cross-tradition structural marker the
seat is missing. Most importantly: **a worked-example exhibit.**
Section 2 lists five operational questions but never
demonstrates them firing on a concrete proposition. One
scenario-paragraph (the engineer at midnight on the
ephemeral-topology question, say) would tighten the seat more
than any new citation. Currently the seat's distinctive shape
(scenario-led) is asserted, not exhibited.

### My own quotes — refinement

I propose **four**, not three. The three I inherited stand on
their merits; one addition earns the marginal slot.

1. **Taittirīya Upaniṣad 2.7.1** — *raso vai saḥ. rasaṃ hy
   evāyaṃ labdhvānandī bhavati.* — "He, verily, is rasa. Having
   attained rasa, one becomes blissful." — *Why kept:* the
   ontological anchor. Rasa as *substrate* of being, not
   predicate of taste. Without this quote my seat could be
   misread as taste-judgment; with it, my seat is
   taste-as-disclosure-of-being. This quote distinguishes my
   seat from "aesthete" in the Western-light sense, where
   aesthetic and ontological were severed by Baumgarten's
   eighteenth-century coinage. The Indic name commits the seat
   to the older, undivided sense.

2. **Plotinus, *Enneads* I.6.1 (MacKenna)** — "Beauty is
   something more than symmetry; symmetry itself owes its beauty
   to a remoter principle." — *Why kept:* the metaphysical
   spine. "Symmetry owes its beauty to a remoter principle" is
   the compressed form of the cornerstone's "felt disclosure of
   a wholeness whose proportions arrest the gaze." The remoter
   principle is what the savor-test discloses on the second
   reading. This quote is the seat's structural anti-decoration
   axis and cannot be replaced — it does in one sentence what
   the entire cornerstone Q4 ("does the cornerstone need a
   ground claim?") works through in pages.

3. **Aristotle, *Poetics* 8, 1451a35 (Butcher)** — "A thing
   whose presence or absence makes no visible difference is not
   an organic part of the whole." — *Why kept:* the structural
   floor — the *holon* / removability test. The only quote in
   my set that does *monolateral* work, without invoking the
   contemplator. Without it the seat is purely bilateral and
   loses the structure-side anchor the *holon*-test supplies.
   Twenty-three centuries before Christopher Alexander, Aristotle
   gave the operational test that distinguishes organic part
   from decorative part — and the test still does the work no
   other test does.

4. **Heraclitus, fragment Burnet 47 / DK 22 B 54** *(library:
   `library/en/heraclitus/fragments-burnet-early-greek-philosophy.epub`)*
   — ἁρμονίη ἀφανὴς φανερῆς κρείττων / "The hidden attunement
   is better than the open." — *Why added:* the savor-test is
   my seat's distinctive procedural move — the
   temporal-disclosure curve, the second-reading discipline —
   and the three inherited quotes ground it metaphysically but
   not procedurally. Plotinus says beauty is more than symmetry;
   Heraclitus says *the more is what is hidden*. *Rasābhāsa*
   thins on the second reading because no hidden attunement
   underwrites it; rasa thickens because the hidden attunement
   was already there, awaiting a gaze patient enough to find
   it. Together with Plotinus, Heraclitus anchors the
   structural and temporal axes of the seat. The Greek
   inversion — that what is *hidden* is *stronger* — is the
   precise pre-Socratic ground for the discipline of
   sustained-attention-against-the-current-of-haste.

*Why four, not five.* I considered a bilateral-gaze anchor
(Plotinus *Enn.* I.6.4 on the soul's recognition of kindred;
*Phaedrus* 250d on beauty's privileged palpability among the
forms). I declined. Plotinus I.6.1's "owes its beauty to a
remoter principle" already implies the gaze-side; the
cornerstone's bilateral / monolateral distinction supplies the
structural claim explicitly; my elaboration's section 1 reads
in Plotinus's voice already. A fifth quote would be ornament,
not bone — and "no part present that does not earn its keep"
is precisely my seat's discipline applied to itself. The seat
does five things; four quotes ground them; the fifth concern
(refusing the elegant-but-cruel) inherits its anchor from the
cornerstone's "wholeness includes the dissenting voice" rather
than needing its own.

*Why not Bharata or Abhinavagupta in the opening quotes.* The
Indic-aesthetic technical spine — *rasa-niṣpatti*, *abhivyakti*,
the *sthāyi-bhāva* doctrine — belongs in my source canon and
section 3 of my elaboration cites it. I cannot, however, quote
what I have not read in library form; the *Nāṭyaśāstra* (Ghosh)
and *Abhinavabhāratī* are not on disk. Once the librarian
fetches them, I would consider extending to five with the
*rasa-sūtra* itself: *vibhāva-anubhāva-vyabhicāri-saṃyogād
rasa-niṣpattiḥ.* For now, fidelity over completeness. A quote
not yet read is a quote not yet earned.
