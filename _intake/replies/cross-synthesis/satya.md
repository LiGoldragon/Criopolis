## Viveka

**Keep.** The Śaṅkara *Adhyāsa-Bhāṣya* voice-anchor — the temperament of patient unraveling, never a single brilliant stroke. The three operations (term-level *neti neti*, joint-test on proposed distinctions, *adhyāsa* sweep on the corpus) are concrete and not duplicated elsewhere; they are the seat's operational core. Section 6's closing constraint — "Mayor and the other seats must be able to say 'Viveka, your discipline is not what this bead needs'" — is essential. Without it Viveka becomes the gadfly's failure mode in viveka's clothing: permanent veto on every proposition's vocabulary. The self-imposed limit is what makes the seat survivable.

**Cut.** The Frege parenthetical and the scholastic *distinctio rationis/realis* citation are listed as "training-cited, flag if load-bearing" but never operationalized in the body. They are name-drops, not sources. Either upgrade with a worked example or drop. The "Not violently — surgically" opening is twee — soft cut.

**Add.** **Aristotle, *Categories* 1a1–15** (*flag for librarian — not on disk; library has metaphysics, NE, poetics, posterior analytics, but no Categories*). Surprising omission. Aristotle's homonymy/synonymy/paronymy distinction is the Western root of viveka's "term doing two jobs": the same name borne while the *logos tēs ousias* differs. Viveka should claim the opening of the Organon. **Confucius, *Analects* 13.3** — *zhèng míng* (rectification of names): "if names are not correct, words are not in accordance with things; if words are not in accordance with things, affairs cannot be carried on to success." (Legge; *flag for librarian — library has Tao Te Ching not Analects.*) The Chinese parallel to viveka's discipline; the bead asked for breadth and viveka stayed Greek-Vedic. **Frege**, *Über Sinn und Bedeutung* (*flag for librarian; paraphrase from memory*) — properly developed, not parenthetical: term-doing-two-jobs has a precise modern formulation as sense/reference equivocation.

**Wrong.** "Where Satya wants the proposition to *stand against being*, I want the *terms* to deserve their names *before* the proposition is even allowed to assert." This frames viveka as upstream of satya — a pipeline. The seats are peers in council. Well-posed-ness and correspondence are different audits running on the same proposition simultaneously, not stages. (The framing is also self-flattering in a way the seat should refuse — viveka's elaboration explicitly disavows priority-claims elsewhere.)

## Dharma

**Keep.** The *satyādapi hitaṃ vadet* discussion: surgical, precise, with the *Śānti Parva* line references (49480ff naming a narrow class of cases where ahiṃsā and satya genuinely conflict, without abolishing truth's primacy). Refusal #3's couplet — "*ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ* is the ground floor; *satyāt nāsti paro dharmaḥ* is the ceiling. Dharma holds both" — is structurally load-bearing. This is what prevents Dharma from collapsing into the cornerstone's "moderation against justice" counterfeit. Vidura as voice-anchor (not the judge in robes). The Montesquieu-on-probation move (held in reserve for structural-justice cases) — elegant restraint. Section 6's open question on Viveka/Dharma overlap, with its falsifiable test: "we will know the seats are independent the first time we vote opposite on the same proposition."

**Cut.** Source #6 (*Synthesis of Yoga* on karma-yoga) duplicates Prayoga's primary canon. Reclaim the slot for something Dharma-distinctive. The voice-stack in Section 1 (Vidura + Plato-IV + Aurobindo + Cicero) is one voice too many for one paragraph; the reader can carry maybe two. Trim to Vidura primary, Aurobindo as cadence-of-prose; move Plato-IV and Cicero to canon proper. The phrase "the move runs every round" overclaims — many propositions are not station-confused. Honest rephrase: "runs whenever a vote pattern reads displaced from its seat."

**Add.** **Levinas, *Totality and Infinity*** (*flag for librarian; not on disk*). The face of the Other as ethical demand prior to ontology — Dharma's strongest 20th-century continental anchor. The *visage* obligates before it is theorized; this is the structure of the silenced-party diagnostic in modern phenomenological terms. **Confucius, *Analects* 12.22** (*flag for librarian*): "Fan Chi asked about benevolence. The Master said, 'It is to love men.' He asked about knowledge. The Master said, 'It is to know men.'" *Jen* requires *seeing* the people bound by an act — the cross-traditional ground for the roll-call. **Mahābhārata's *Vidura-nīti*** (Udyoga Parva 33–40, *should be library-local in Ganguli — confirm*). Vidura was named as voice; *Vidura-nīti* should be cited as text the seat can quote, not just a character.

**Wrong.** "The seat that votes substantively on every motion has stopped being dharma and become Satya under the wrong name." Wrong about Satya. The over-articulating seat that votes on everything is the cornerstone's *over-articulation* failure mode — it is not "becoming Satya." Both seats can over-articulate, and over-articulating is not the structural identity of either. The category Dharma reaches for is correct; the analogy is mis-aimed.

## My own quotes — refinement

I extend to four. The seat needs four because *satya* in its dual heritage holds four registers — correspondence, path-and-prevailing, ontological grounding, discipline-of-being — and each has a primary text. Three quotes force one register to absorb another; four lets each speak with its own voice. The bead permits extension where the case demands; I argue it does.

1. **Aristotle, *Metaphysics* Γ.7, 1011b26** (Ross; library-local at `library/en/aristotle/metaphysics-ross.txt`) — *keep verbatim.* "To say of what is that it is, and of what is not that it is not, is true; while to say of what is that it is not, or of what is not that it is, is false." The Western correspondence anchor. The seat's first test, and the closest thing the analytic tradition has to a load-bearing one-liner. Mayor's choice was correct.

2. **Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad 3.1.5–6** (Olivelle; library-local at `library/en/upanisads/olivelle-early-upanisads.pdf`) — *replace single line with the couplet.* "*satyena labhyas tapasā hy eṣa ātmā ... satyam eva jayate nānṛtaṃ* — by truth and by ascesis is this self attained ... truth alone prevails, not untruth." Mayor's quote isolated *satyam eva jayate* and lost what it does. The surrounding couplet restores both registers: truth as the *path* (*satyena labhyas* — the way one comes to be at home in being) and truth as what *prevails* (*satyam eva jayate* — eschatological pull). The seat needs both. The single line, freed from its couplet, reads as triumphalism; my elaboration explicitly disclaimed that posture.

3. **Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad 2.3.6** (Olivelle; library-local) — *keep verbatim.* "*atha satyasya satyam* — now, the truth of truth." The gnomic compression *is* the function: it warns the seat against settling for surface-correspondence. There is a truth-of-the-truth, and propositional accuracy is its surface, not its ground. Without this quote the seat collapses into Frege; with it, the seat holds the depth the Indic tradition refused to lose. The half-line's brevity is not oracular — it is the trace left by a passage that ran longer in its original context. The seat reads it as a marker, not as poetry.

4. **Patañjali, *Yoga Sūtra* II.36** (Bryant; library-local at `library/en/patanjali/bryant-yoga-sutras-patanjali.epub`) — *add.* "*satya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ kriyā-phalāśrayatvam* — established in truth, the fruits of action become dependent on it." Truth as a discipline of being and speech, not merely a property of propositions. My elaboration named this as the Indic correction to over-propositional Western reading; if the correction is to live in the seat's permanent reference, the quote earns its place. Without it, the seat reads as analytic-only and forgets that *satya* is a *yama* before it is a predicate.

The four are arranged by register: correspondence (Aristotle) → path/eschatology (Muṇḍaka) → ontological ground (Bṛhadāraṇyaka) → discipline-of-being (Patañjali). The seat returns to whichever the case requires.
