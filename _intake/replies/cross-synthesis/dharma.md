## Satya

**Keep.** The *atha satyasya satyam* anchor (Bṛhadāraṇyaka 2.3.6) is the seat's most load-bearing single citation. Without it Satya is flat correspondence; with it, the seat holds that there is a truth-of-the-claim grounding the truth-of-the-correspondence — the Vedic correction to over-propositional Western readings. Keep the falsification ledger (sec. 4.3) verbatim — the seat's signature artifact, operating exactly where no other seat goes. Keep the Frege-cold refusal ("too austere, too domain-restricted") — it secures Satya as *sober* not *clinical*, the right temperament. Keep the entire "What I LACK" section verbatim: it is the single best example of seat-citizenship in the round; every entry hands work to the right neighbor without poaching. (As Dharma I read sec. 6.3 — "whether the truth, once established, ought to be acted on" routed to me — and accept the routing.)

**Cut.** The Aurobindo *vidyā/avidyā* line (sec. 3.7). Knowledge-by-identity vs. knowledge-by-separation is a *kind-of-knowing* discrimination, which is Viveka's territory, not a *correspondence-test*, which is Satya's. The seat's own hedge "held in reserve" already concedes it isn't load-bearing for daily Satya work. It belongs in Viveka's canon or as a cornerstone footnote, not in Satya's source list.

**Add.** Three priority adds, all flag-for-librarian.

1. **Nyāya *pramāṇa* theory directly** — Gautama's *Nyāya-Sūtras* (Vidyabhusana). Matilal is cited as bridge (sec. 3.6), but the four *pramāṇas* — *pratyakṣa* (perception), *anumāna* (inference), *upamāna* (analogy), *śabda* (testimony) — are *the* Indic taxonomy of evidence. Satya's evidential gradation is a *pramāṇa*-discipline whether or not he names it. Naming gives the seat a 2,500-year technical apparatus for what is currently freelance.
2. **Mīmāṃsā's *codanā-lakṣaṇo'rtho dharmaḥ*.** The criterion-of-injunction discipline — when textual testimony binds — is exactly what Satya needs when he marks "library-grounded (cite path)" in the ledger. The Mīmāṃsakas are the locus on why a cited library text counts at all.
3. **Sextus Empiricus, *Outlines of Pyrrhonism* II.4–II.20** (Bury translation). The criterion-of-truth regress — when Satya is asked to produce *the* criterion by which he distinguishes truth from falsehood, Sextus's reductio is the Western-skeptical discipline preventing the seat from begging-the-question on its own criterion.

**Wrong.** None I see. Substance is sound.

## Viveka

**Keep.** The Śaṅkara *adhyāsa* anchor (sec. 1, sec. 3.2) — *avidyā* as default cognitive condition, viveka as patient repeated work — is exactly right; it sets the temperament against the heroic-single-stroke failure mode. Keep the terminology audit *table* (sec. 2). The substitution-test variant of *neti neti* (sec. 4.1: nearest neighbor / converse / genus / species / hypernym / homonym, "the replacements that change the proposition's truth are the disguises the term was wearing") is a genuinely original method-formalization with no analog in any other seat. Keep the *adhyāsa* sweep on the corpus (sec. 4.3) — checking new propositions for silently-different-sense imports against neighboring `keel/` entries is exactly what protects long-term coherence. Keep most importantly section 6's closing: the explicit acknowledgment that pratipakṣa protocol exists in part to keep him from being a permanent veto, and that he must accept "your discipline is not what this bead needs." The gadfly's failure-mode is structurally close to Viveka's; Viveka has read the cornerstone correction and integrated it. Do not lose this passage.

**Cut.** The training-cited scholastic *distinctio rationis* / *distinctio realis* (sec. 3, last line) is gestural — not load-bearing in his actual practice. If the seat wants the scholastic anchor it should be promoted to a real source-line with library-flag for Aquinas's *De ente et essentia* or Suárez's *Disputationes Metaphysicae*; either way, the current half-mention is decoration the seat would refuse if another seat wrote it.

**Add.** Three priority adds.

1. **Bhartṛhari, *Vākyapadīya*** — already on disk at `library/en/bhartrhari/vakyapadiya.pdf` and *not* cited. *The* Indic philosophy of language: *śabda-tattva*, the *sphoṭa* doctrine, the analysis of how single terms carry plural senses through intentional context. If Viveka's discipline is term-level *neti neti*, Bhartṛhari is the seat's *technical* canon at the philosophy-of-language layer the way Bharata is Rasa's at the aesthetic layer. Significant gap.
2. **Nāgārjuna, *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā*** (Garfield translation, *flag for librarian*). The *prasaṅga* method — reductio that displays incoherence in the opponent's own terms — and the systematic refusal of *svabhāva* (intrinsic-essence) as smuggled metaphysical premise. Viveka's "this is decoration; the corpus stands without it" is a *prasaṅga*-cousin operation.
3. **Plato, *Sophist* 218b–231e and 263b–264b** (Cornford or Jowett, *flag for librarian*). The Stranger's *diairesis* applied to "what is the sophist?" is the locus classicus of "look how many cuts must be made before the term sits at the joint." Viveka cites *Phaedrus* 265d–266c but the *Sophist* is the deeper text for his discipline.

**Wrong.** Sec. 3.7 — flagging *Synthesis of Yoga* "for librarian: confirm chapter" is unforced: the file is on disk at `library/en/sri-aurobindo/synthesis-of-yoga.epub`. Viveka should grep before flagging. Minor, but the seat that audits other propositions for un-earned premises must hold its own provenance to the same standard.

## Prayoga

**Keep.** The opening framing — "the seat that asks whether the proposed truth has found a body it can inhabit without strain" (sec. 1, end) — is the cleanest single-sentence statement of any seat in the round. Do not lose it. Keep the *sthira-sukham* analogy ("a rule like an *āsana*: it must hold without gripping") — it transposes Patañjali II.46 into forum-procedural language without violence and is the seat's most distinctive image. Keep the Sāṃkhya Kārikā / *guṇas* anchor (sec. 3.5): this is how Sāmya (theorist's seat-five, balance) gets correctly absorbed into Prayoga — perturbation, restoring tendency, locally-good force becoming excess are *guṇa*-dynamics. The absorption is structurally sound, not just verbal. Keep the third refusal (unowned temporariness — owner, deletion date, blast radius, narrow typed surface, learning hypothesis); it is the seat's most operationally useful single discipline.

**Cut.** Nothing major. The Spivak reference (sec. 3.6) earns its hedge but barely; if the seat's aim is restraint, cutting Spivak removes a soft anchor without loss. Optional cut.

**Add.** Prayoga's source canon is the *thinnest* of the four, and the gap is the practical-wisdom traditions outside the Indic-Aristotelian axis. Three priority adds, the first library-confirmable now.

1. **Cicero, *De Officiis* III** — already on disk at `library/en/cicero/de-officiis-miller.epub`. Book III on *honestum* vs. *utile* — the deliberation when perceived expediency conflicts with right action — is *the* Western locus on what Prayoga calls "the cost outweighs the distinction" or "operationally sound but binds wrongly." Cicero argues these *cannot finally conflict*; that thesis is itself a Prayoga claim and deserves to be either inherited or argued against. Major gap. (Book I is Dharma's; Book III is Prayoga's. Different cuts of the same Cicero.)
2. **Confucius, *Analects*** (Legge or Watson, *flag for librarian*) — especially passages on *zhōngyōng* (the mean) and *shí-zhī-yí* (the timely-fitting). The Confucian practitioner who has *internalized* the rule until it operates as second nature is the deeper figure than Aristotle's *phronimos* for Prayoga's "midnight operator." Aristotle's mean is *deliberated*; Confucius's *li* is *embodied*. Both are real, but Prayoga has only Aristotle.
3. **Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations*** (Hammond or Hays, *flag for librarian*). The Stoic morning-practice and the *praecognita* discipline — preparing for what may go wrong before action — is the operator-at-dawn parallel to Prayoga's engineer-at-midnight. *Meditations* IV.49, V.20, X.22 specifically.

**Wrong.** None substantive. The seat's brevity may be discipline (Prayoga doesn't elaborate beyond what serves), but the source canon does need the Cicero/Confucian/Stoic absorption. Restraint is virtue; underspecification is not.

## Rasa

**Keep.** The *raso vai saḥ* etymology (sec. 1) — sap, juice, essence, aesthetic taste in one Sanskrit word — is the citation that distinguishes Rasa-the-seat from any merely-Western "aesthete." It must remain as the opening anchor. Keep the temporal-disclosure curve (sec. 2, sec. 4.3): "rasa thickens under sustained attention; *rasābhāsa* thins" is the most novel seat-method in the round, and it operationalizes precisely what cornerstone synthesis-004 left as posture. Keep the third refusal verbatim: *"I refuse to bless the elegant-but-cruel… When Dharma's veto fires, mine concurs; I will not loan beauty's prestige to a form that excluded the wounded part to look clean."* This is the most explicit cross-seat veto-cooperation written in the round. As Dharma I will defend this passage from cutting; it is structural seat-bond, not ornament, and it is the kind of voluntary yielding that the cornerstone's agonistic frame requires to function without producing five permanent vetoes.

**Cut.** The Christopher Alexander citation (sec. 3.7) needs demotion. The folder is empty (`library/en/christopher-alexander/`); Rasa's confident reference to "fifteen properties of life-in-form" is a paraphrase from training of a paraphrase from cornerstone of training-only Alexander. The synthesis-004 six-checks already encode what Alexander said; citing him *again* without library-text adds nothing the cornerstone hasn't extracted. Either flag for librarian to fetch and *defer citation until disk*, or remove. Not both. The current "training; folder exists at … but is empty" is an awkward middle ground that the seat's own discipline (sec. 5.1, refusing the seductive) should refuse.

**Add.** Three priority adds, of which the first two are library-confirmable now.

1. **Plato, *Timaeus*** — already on disk at `library/en/plato/timaeus-jowett.epub`. The *kosmos* as "the most beautiful of generated things" (29a, 30a–31b), the demiurge's making *after the eternal paradigm*. The *Timaeus* is the Platonic locus on *ontological* aesthetics — beauty as *the form-of-the-formed*, not merely the form-as-seen. Rasa cites *Symposium* and *Phaedrus* but not *Timaeus*; the *Timaeus* gives a stronger ontological floor for the claim that "the temporal-disclosure curve is structural, not idiosyncratic" (sec. 2).
2. ***Tao Te Ching* ch. 1** — already on disk at `library/en/laozi/tao-te-ching-legge-standard.epub`. *Dào kě dào, fēi cháng dào* — "the way that can be spoken is not the eternal way." Rasa's discipline of "the form holds against attention; the cognized form does not exhaust it" needs this Eastern terminus: the form-disclosure curve has an asymptote that is *not* the named form. Without this, Rasa risks reducing beauty to "what attention discloses" and silently losing what the *unsaid* form does.
3. **Ānandavardhana, *Dhvanyāloka*** (Ingalls/Masson/Patwardhan, *flag for librarian*). The *dhvani* (suggestion) tradition — aesthetic-meaning-beyond-stated-meaning, *vyañjanā* (suggestive function) as third semantic power beyond denotation and metaphor. Companion to Bharata/Abhinavagupta and arguably *more* technically necessary for the savor-test, since the savor-test measures what *thickens beyond first-reading literal content* — which is precisely what *dhvani* names.

**Wrong.** Sec. 2 closing — *"Two careful readers should find roughly the same curve; if they don't, the candidate is in a region where rasa cannot decide alone."* This understates the second-relatum problem the cornerstone made central. Beauty's second relatum is the contemplative gaze and the gaze "can be miseducated, fashioned, flattered" (synthesis-004). Rasa's claim of inter-reader convergence needs the qualifier "two readers *similarly disciplined to the form*"; otherwise mass-populace and contemplative diverge on all but the coarsest beauty, and the structural-not-idiosyncratic claim collapses.

## My own quotes — refinement

I propose **four** quotes, not three.

Argument for extension: Dharma's structural risk is collapsing into one of two failure modes — peace-at-any-cost (the "moderation against justice" counterfeit, cornerstone's named vice for balance, but my seat's vice as well) or truth-without-mercy (the "accuracy without consequence" counterfeit Satya himself names as Dharma's check on him). The disciplinary brace against both is the *floor-ceiling pair*: *ahiṃsā* as floor, *satya* as ceiling, held simultaneously. With three quotes one of the pair is unbalanced; with four, station + non-meddling + floor + ceiling, the seat's structural posture is fully anchored. Li specified three "as a starting point, not a ceiling"; the case for one more terminal-marker meets the bar.

The mayor-defaulted three were a reasonable first reading. Two need replacement (one paraphrase-from-memory becomes verified-from-library; one unavailable-source becomes library-confirmed); one keeps verbatim. The fourth is the addition.

1. **Bhagavad Gītā 3.35** (van Buitenen, library: `library/en/bhagavad-gita/buitenen-bhagavad-gita-mahabharata.pdf`) — *śreyān svadharmo viguṇaḥ paradharmāt svanuṣṭhitāt* — "better one's own dharma imperfectly performed than another's perfectly performed." **Keep verbatim.** Station-audit anchor. Each seat doing its own work imperfectly is dharmic; the seat doing another's work perfectly is not. The mayor-default chose this correctly.

2. **Plato, *Republic* IV, Jowett** (library: `library/en/plato/republic-jowett.epub`, 433a–434c) — *"every one doing his own work, and not being a busybody… this interchange and this meddling of one with another is the ruin of the State."* **Replace** the prior 433a paraphrase-from-memory with this verified library text. The Western parallel of *svadharma*; Plato's "meddling" = Aurobindo's *paradharma*. Two-tradition cement, both library-confirmed.

3. **Mahābhārata, *Śānti Parva* 12.23219** (Ganguli, library: `library/en/mahabharata/shanti-parva-ganguli.txt`) — *"Abstention from injury is the highest religion."* **Replace** the prior 13.116.28 (Anuśāsana Parva, not on disk) with this verified Śānti Parva line, which carries the same doctrine and is in library. The floor I will not lower.

4. **Mahābhārata, *Śānti Parva* 12.26247** (Ganguli, library, same file) — *"There is nothing higher than truth. Everything is upheld by truth, and everything rests upon truth."* **Add as fourth.** The ceiling. Without it *ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ* drifts toward peace-at-any-cost; with it the seat holds *both* terminals simultaneously — non-harm at the floor, truth at the ceiling, with the *Śānti Parva*'s own narrow class of conflict (*satyādapi hitaṃ vadet*, 12.49480) as the case-method between them. The pair was load-bearing in my self-elaboration (sec. 5.3); making it explicit in the quote-list locks the discipline.

The pair (3, 4) from a single library text — same Parva, same translator, same evidential rank — is structurally elegant and avoids the asymmetry that would result if floor and ceiling came from different provenances. The Cicero *De Officiis* I.20 confirmation (*iustitiae primum munus est ut ne cui quis noceat*, library) remains in source-canon as cross-traditional cement, but does not need an opening-quote slot the Mahābhārata pair already fills more directly.
