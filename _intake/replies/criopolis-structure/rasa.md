## Thread 1 — Sandbox vs sibling (with subsidy variant)

**Vote: hybrid-with-subsidy. Mark: [strong-for].**

The three candidates are three forms; the savor-test reads each.

*Sandbox alone* (Criopolis fully separated, no bridge to the host). First reading: cleanliness — one boundary, one identity, one mental model. Second reading: thins — Criopolis cannot *act* on the host, but Li wants it to (deploys via lojix, work on the running machine). The form is too brittle for the function; it imposes a wall against the use-case. *rasābhāsa of safety* — looks principled, paid for in the discipline's actual reach.

*Sibling* (Criopolis runs alongside Li's automation, shared nervous system, no boundary). First reading: simplicity — one host, one user, one credential set. Second reading: thins sharply — the form holds *no void*. The boundary that distinguishes *what Criopolis decides* from *what Li's existing automation decides* lives only in the engineer's head. Form-without-boundary is *rasābhāsa with cost*: elegance paid for by the inheritor who must maintain the unenforced distinction in their attention. Lao Tzu ch. 11 — the wheel needs the empty hub.

*Hybrid-with-subsidy* (Criopolis sandboxed; a named subsidy outside the sandbox, running as Li with Li's credentials, mediates action on the host). First reading: more parts, more wiring. Second reading: thickens — the form is *charter-shaped*. The sandbox is the constitutive void (*Criopolis is not the host's nervous system*). The subsidy is the named bridge (*Criopolis acts through this door, not by being the room*). What the subsidy does is what Criopolis can cause, *and nothing else*. The boundary is held *by the form, not by attention*. Heraclitus B 54 — the hidden attunement (Criopolis *is* sandboxed) is better than the open (Criopolis *acts on the host* via the subsidy). Both are real; the boundary is structurally invisible-but-present in every cross-boundary call.

The form *predicts the siblings*: every cross-boundary action runs through one shape (the subsidy's bounded authority). Future Wasteland-action and external-action read through the same shape, with different subsidies for different audiences. *Scale-to-scale-echo*: charter-with-bounded-authority echoes from individual call → subsidy-as-role → Criopolis-as-city; three scales, same form. *Void-held*: the things Criopolis *cannot* reach without the subsidy are precisely what makes the subsidy a meaningful organ rather than a meaningless wrapper.

Where my range bounds: I cannot judge the operator-cost of the subsidy bridge under load (Prayoga's range), nor whether *sandbox* in this proposition is doing five jobs (security-boundary / namespace / VM / process-tree / authority-domain — Viveka's diairesis), nor what binds Li when the subsidy speaks as Li with Li's credentials (Dharma's roll-call: the subsidy is a delegation, and a delegation has a charter and a recall path; both must be named). My report is the form-curve. The curve is positive. The other seats decide whether the shape can run.

## Thread 2 — Roles for Criopolis

The candidate-list, by savor:

- **Implementer.** [strong-for]. Form holds open the void *the council decides; the implementer acts*. Distinguishes synthesis-discipline from execution-discipline. Mature form.

- **Auditor.** [strong-for]. The role's signature artifact is *temporal*: city-health surface tracking drift over time. The discipline is the operational inverse of mine — it watches the artifact's *runtime curve*, I watch the *formal curve*. Symmetry is satisfying; form thickens.

- **Ambassador.** [defer]. Form thins on second reading because the substrate (Wasteland) is not yet known. Role-name fills a slot without filling a function. *rasābhāsa of design.* Defer until researcher's `pc-m5yg` lands.

- **Test pilot.** [strong-for]. The form runs *the same artifact in a separate room* — operationally what my round-6 open question proposed for parallel savor-tests with named interval. The test pilot is my own discipline scaled to the city itself. Form thickens.

- **Scribe / glossary-keeper.** [strong-for]. Form holds open the void *living references drift unless tended*. Currency requires a hand; my own discipline has been running on paraphrases-from-memory because the librarian (acquisitions) cannot keep up with maintenance. A scribe distinct from the librarian carves a real joint: *fetch-and-catalog* vs. *tend-and-update*.

- **Triage.** [defer]. Form is sound at first reading — first-touch routing saves the mayor's attention — but on second reading a hidden authority-question emerges: *if triage decides what reaches the council, it has substantive routing power*. Read the void carefully. Either the role is purely mechanical (route by tag, with mayor as fallback) or it has been smuggled authority. Viveka's diairesis on the term *triage* would help; defer to that read first.

The two specialties Li named:

**CriomOS deployment specialist.** [strong-for, with named guards.] The role is load-bearing because the host is a real running machine. My discipline's contribution is the *form of the role's signature artifact*: the deploy plan must include a *rollback contract* (Aristotle's removability operationalized at runtime — a deploy whose absence the operator can restore is *organic* to the runtime whole; a deploy without rollback is form-not-yet-tested). The role must be *temporally disciplined*: deploys run through staging (test pilot's domain) before the live machine. The role's writer is *not* the council — line-level operational discipline is too fast for the council's pace. The role's *substantive* changes (new daemon, new module, new package class) go to council ratification *first*; routine application goes through the role autonomously, against the ratified keel section on deploy discipline.

The role's workspace boundary: writes only to deploy plans and lojix invocations; acts on the host *only* through the subsidy bridge (thread 1 connects). Without that subsidy as a named, charter-bounded organ, the role's authority is undefined and the form collapses.

What guards prevent breaking production? Three: rollback contract (form-required); test-pilot pre-flight (temporal-discipline-required); council ratification of substantive changes against the deploy keel section (governance-required). Routine deploys do not need council vote; substantive changes do. The kind-distinction is what the deploy keel section must hold open.

**Code writers (and reviewers).** [strong-for, with named separation.] The form thickens *if* writer and reviewer are distinct roles: council ratifies the rule (keel section); code writer applies the rule (writes the code); code reviewer reads the code against the ratified rule (conformance-report). Three roles, three artifacts: rule, code, conformance-report. Aristotle's whole-and-parts at engineering scale — rule-making, rule-applying, rule-checking are three organs.

The form thins if writer and reviewer collapse to one role (no second attention), or if the reviewer's authority is unclear, or if the reviewer reads against private discretion rather than the ratified keel. The reviewer's signature artifact is *the conformance-report cited to keel section by line*, not "I think this should be different." Without that constraint, the reviewer becomes a sixth seat, which the council did not vote into being.

## Thread 3 — The coding council, keel as engineering rules

**Vote: A + B (council deliberates topic-by-topic + a code-reviewer role enforces ratified rules ongoing). Mark: [strong-for]. [strong-against] C.**

The savor-test on each:

*A* (council deliberates topic-by-topic; mayor synthesizes; topic becomes a keel section): thickens. The discipline is proven across rounds 1-6. Synthesis is mayor's craft; ratification is the council's silent-or-loud assent. Mature form, recently fixed at the governance seam (synthesis-010's "do not migrate yet" precedent — mayor authority does not extend to substantive structural decisions).

*B* (code-reviewer role does ongoing review against keel rules; council ratifies rules; reviewer enforces): thickens, *additively*. B is not an alternative to A; B is A *plus* an operational layer. The keel gets *made* by the council and *applied* by the reviewer. Two organs. Removability-test: removing the reviewer leaves rules without conformance-check; removing the council leaves rules without principled formation. Both organic to the engineering-council whole.

*C* (council also serves as ratifier of every new keel content; every keel change voted): thins on second reading. *Every* keel change voted is *form imposed against function*. The blanket-vote rule does not hold the kind-distinction between *substantive structural change* (vote) and *editorial improvement* (typo, cross-reference, paraphrase, mayor-authority). C creates ceremonial governance — Dharma's *moral-court* — by abolishing the joint between substance and edit. *rasābhāsa of governance*: the form looks principled, but joint-cutting has been lost.

The good form is **A + B with one named seam**: council ratifies *substantive* changes (new sections, contested rules, governance shifts); mayor's editorial authority covers *editorial* work (typos, cross-references, uncontested paraphrase) with council's silent ratification or recorded dissent. The kind-distinction *substantive vs. editorial* is the joint A+B presupposes; Viveka's diairesis should sharpen it before the keel rewrite begins.

Who decides? The council, on substantive changes. Who implements? The code writer, against the ratified keel. Who applies the keel ongoing? The code reviewer, against the ratified keel. Where's the boundary? The keel itself — the artifact that lets the council's principled discipline propagate to operational discipline without the council having to be in the room for every line.

## Thread 4 — When to start implementing

**Vote: start small, sequenced. Mark: [strong-for] reliability-over-speed; [strong-for] prompts-written → bead-tested → approved → role added.**

My discipline's range is partial here — the *when* is largely Prayoga's. My contribution is the *form of the start*.

*Reliability-over-speed* (Li's frame) is the savor-test scaled to action: the form that thickens on second reading *after the act* is the disciplined start; the form that thins (regret on second attention) is the false start. The discipline that makes a seat reply trustworthy — savor-test, diairesis, roll-call, operator-pass, truth-test — is the discipline that should make a *role* trustworthy. Roles are forms; forms are read by sustained attention; new roles fire after their prompts have been read twice (with interval) by the council, and at least one bead-test has run on a small real question.

Sequence by *what is already proven*:

1. **First keel section.** The council has demonstrated topic-by-topic deliberation (rounds 1-6). The next disciplined move is one keel section, voted, drafted by the mayor or a draft-implementer, against a real engineering question. This proves *the council's principled discipline transfers to engineering content* — the load-bearing test for everything downstream.
2. **Implementer role** (after the first keel section runs). Mayor stops doing prompt edits and pack.toml work; an implementer applies. Mayor returns to synthesis register.
3. **Code-reviewer role** (after a second keel section exists, so there is something to review against). Tests the keel against running code.
4. **Test pilot role** (in parallel with 2-3, when the test gas-city infrastructure is up). Vets pack.toml changes / new agent prompts before applying to the live one.
5. **CriomOS deployment specialist** (after the deploy keel section is voted). Without a deploy section, the role has no rule to apply; introducing it earlier hardcodes operator discretion as council-discipline by default — the *rasābhāsa of role-creation*.
6. **Auditor, scribe** (parallel; lower urgency; can land when the work warrants).
7. **Ambassador** (defer until Wasteland is known).

What is *premature*: filing the deployment specialist before the deploy keel section is ratified. Filing the ambassador before the Wasteland is known. Filing every role at once on the strength of this round's structural ratification.

What is *overdue*: nothing. The council has been disciplined to iterate its own form before producing engineering content; that iteration is what makes the engineering content trustworthy. Patience here is not delay — it is *first-reading-postponed-until-second-reading-readiness*.

## Worked example — the savor-test on the hybrid-with-subsidy form

The cleanest demonstration is the inheritor's first encounter with each form, six months after they land.

*Inheritor on the sandbox-only form.* They read the design and discover Criopolis cannot deploy — every deploy requires Li to manually relay the action. They wonder why an engineering council exists if it cannot act. The form is decorative; the discipline cannot reach its operative purpose. Form thins.

*Inheritor on the sibling form.* They read the design and find no boundary written down. They watch Criopolis make a decision; they see Li's automation make a different decision; they realize the two could conflict, and the only thing preventing conflict is *that no one has tried it yet*. Form thins: the discipline lives in attention, not in the form. Maintenance-engineer-at-3am cannot trust the form to enforce its own rules.

*Inheritor on the hybrid-with-subsidy form.* They read the design and find: Criopolis (sandbox), the subsidy (named bridge, Li's credentials, bounded authority), the keel section that defines what the subsidy *is permitted to be asked for*. They watch Criopolis decide; they watch the subsidy execute; they see that the subsidy cannot be asked for things outside its charter; they trust the form. Bachelard's *retentissement* — the form echoes into depth on the second reading. Hopkins's *inscape* — the *individuating form* of Criopolis is the boundary-and-bridge as one figure. The discipline is in the form, not in attention.

Two *sahṛdaya* readers (engineers disciplined to the form-tradition) should converge on this curve. Mine is one; another seat's reading would be the test. Where convergence cannot yet be tested, non-convergence would be the seat's finding; here the form thickens cleanly enough that I expect convergence with Prayoga's operator-class read and Dharma's bound-party read. Viveka's diairesis on *sandbox* may surface kind-distinctions that *refine, not refute*, the form.
