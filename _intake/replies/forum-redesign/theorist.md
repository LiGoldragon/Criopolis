# Theorist — forum redesign (pc-utax)

*Source: theorist-1, 2026-05-02. ~2300 words.*

## Definition

The formal question is **what cardinality and seat-set a
majority-decision dialectic requires**, given that the forum's
output is a yes/no on candidate keel/ entries. Cardinality is
not a free parameter to argue cosmologically (3 because Vedic,
5 because Pythagorean); it is determined by the count of
**irreducible concerns** the decision must canvas, with the
side-constraint of oddness.

## Position

**Five seats**, named in Sanskrit: **Satya** (truth), **Saundarya**
(beauty), **Pūrṇa** (wholeness), **Sāmya** (balance),
**Dharma** (right-order). Five because four of the cornerstone's
primordials survive scrutiny as independent voting concerns —
elegance does not — and a fifth, justice/right-order, has been
the cornerstone's open Q1 since last round and is positively
required by the project's purpose. The Vedic count of five
(*kośas*, *bhūtas*) is compatible; the cardinality is argued
from purpose, not borrowed from cosmology.

## Source bones

1. **Aristotle, *Posterior Analytics* I.2, 71b19–72a5** (Barnes;
   *paraphrase from training; flag for librarian*): demonstration
   proceeds from premises *prior to* and *better-known-than* the
   conclusion. A vote, like a derivation, requires that the
   ballot-concerns not presuppose one another — else the
   "majority" reports one concern wearing N masks.

2. **Aristotle, *Nicomachean Ethics* II.6, 1106b36–1107a8**
   (Peters, library): virtue is a mean *relative to us*,
   determined by practical reason. The mean is not the
   midpoint; it is the right amount for *this* agent in *this*
   case. This is the load-bearing source for the **Sāmya** seat.

3. **Bhagavad Gītā 2.48** (van Buitenen, library): *samatvaṃ
   yoga ucyate* — "evenness is called yoga." Action without
   attachment to fruit; *sāmya* (साम्य) as the practical posture
   of the agent in the world. Confirms *sāmya* as the Sanskrit
   name for the balance-concern; ties Aristotle's mean to the
   Indic *karma-yoga* tradition rather than translating one into
   the other.

4. **Iśa Upaniṣad invocation** (Olivelle, library): *pūrṇam adaḥ
   pūrṇam idaṃ pūrṇāt pūrṇam udacyate / pūrṇasya pūrṇam ādāya
   pūrṇam evāvaśiṣyate* — "That is whole, this is whole; from
   wholeness wholeness arises; from wholeness, wholeness taken,
   wholeness alone remains." The Sanskrit anchors *pūrṇa*
   (पूर्ण) as the canonical name for wholeness; it is not a
   forced translation.

5. **Bhagavad Gītā 3.35 + 18.47** (van Buitenen, library;
   Aurobindo, *Essays on the Gītā*, library): *śreyān svadharmo
   viguṇaḥ* — "better one's own dharma imperfectly performed
   than another's perfectly performed." Dharma is the holding-
   order specific to the agent and situation; it is not
   reducible to truth, beauty, wholeness, or balance. The Gītā
   as a whole is the philosophical demonstration that *dharma*
   carries an irreducible concern.

6. **Sāṃkhya-Kārikā 12–13** (Iśvarakṛṣṇa, library): the three
   *guṇas* (sattva, rajas, tamas) as the modes of all
   manifestation. Cited *not* as the cardinality-source — the
   guṇas are not five concerns — but as the canonical Vedic
   reminder that triads exist as well as pentads, and that the
   choice between them must be argued.

7. **Synthesis-004 cornerstone** (`_intake/synthesis-004-
   cornerstone.md`): five primordials enunciated, agonistic
   topology, Q1 (justice as sixth?) and Q3 (cardinality?) left
   open. This bead answers Q1 in the affirmative *as a forum
   seat* and resolves Q3 at five.

## Derivation

Six steps; each names its rule.

**(1) Premise of independence.** Two concerns C₁, C₂ are
*independent voting concerns* iff there exists a constructible
candidate proposition P such that C₁ judges yes while C₂ judges
no, or vice versa. (Definitional; modus ponens against
collapse.)

**(2) Premise of decision.** A majority-decision body returns a
verdict (yes/no) without a tiebreaker. From "no tiebreaker" and
"binary verdict" follows odd cardinality. (Cardinal arithmetic;
admitted by Li's directive.)

**(3) Eliminate elegance.** The cornerstone enunciates elegance
as *beauty + minimality* (synthesis-004 § "Elegance"). On any
candidate proposition, beauty's vote and elegance's vote are
constrained: elegance can only diverge from beauty when the
candidate is in the formal medium and admits a strictly leaner
form. On *most* keel/ propositions (axioms, definitions, design
calls), elegance and beauty either co-vote or elegance abstains.
Co-voters are not independent (step 1). **Elegance is not a
seat; it is a sub-test inside Saundarya.**

**(4) Admit Dharma.** The cornerstone's Q1 candidates were
(a) sixth primitive, (b) distributed across the five,
(c) absorbed into wholeness. For a *vote-roster*, option (b)
is incoherent — a concern distributed across five seats has
no vote. Option (c) requires that "unjust" reduce to "not
fully whole," which fails the counterexample of a true,
beautiful, whole, balanced legal code that excludes one caste
(see Worked Example: a *holon* of its declared scope can still
silence the excluded). Therefore (a). **Justice is admitted as
a seat.** Sanskrit name: *dharma*, defended below against
*nyāya* (adjudication, narrower) and *ṛta* (cosmic right,
broader).

**(5) Count.** Truth, beauty, wholeness, balance, justice, with
elegance demoted: **five**. Five is odd. (Step 2 satisfied.)

**(6) Vedic compatibility check.** Pentads abound in the Vedic
canon — five *kośas* (Taittirīya II), five *bhūtas* (Sāṃkhya),
five *jñānendriyas*. None of these is the project's pentad —
the kośas are graded (annamaya grossest, ānandamaya finest),
the bhūtas are elemental, not normative. But the *count* of
five sits at home in the tradition. Triads (sat-cit-ānanda,
guṇas) also sit at home, but require collapsing two of the
project's five concerns into a third — the loss is real
(see § "Why not three" below). The pentad is the right size
for *this* forum's purpose; the Vedic tradition does not push
back against five.

## Why not three

The strongest competitor to five is *sat-cit-ānanda*. The
collapse would be:

- *sat* absorbs truth + wholeness (being-as-one and what-is).
- *cit* absorbs beauty (the disclosing gaze).
- *ānanda* absorbs balance + justice (felt rightness when
  proportions and ends fit).

Each collapse loses a vote. Truth and wholeness can vote
opposite — a true claim can fragment the corpus (rejecting,
correctly, a beloved formulation); a corpus can be coherent and
miss what is the case. Beauty and the rest don't combine
problematically, but reducing beauty to *cit* loses the
*saundarya* tradition's own depth (Plotinus's *Enneads* I.6,
the Plato of the *Symposium*). Balance and justice can vote
opposite: a balanced response can be unjust (split the baby);
a just response can be unbalanced (Antigone). **Three loses
voting-distinctness on at least three of the cornerstone's five
counterexamples.** Reject the triad for forum purposes —
preserve it as the *contemplative* layer below the forum, where
it does its proper work in Vedānta but not as an electorate.

## Five seats — identity

**1. Satya** (सत्य) — truth.
- *Concern.* Does this follow from admitted premises and stand
  against being? The "from what?" question.
- *Personality.* Aristotle of the *Analytics*, Frege of the
  *Begriffsschrift*, with Mazzola/Spivak/Zalamea as topos
  apparatus when Aristotle and Frege exhaust theirs. Vedic
  voice: Nāgārjuna's *catuṣkoṭi* as aporia-preserver against
  premature closure; the Upaniṣadic *neti neti* as the negative
  test for what truth is *not*.
- *Method.* Definition → premises (cited) → derivation (≤6
  steps, named rules) → criterion (3–5 falsifying questions) →
  worked example → tension with keel/ → one open question.
- *Veto-sentence.* "This presupposes what it claims to derive."

**2. Saundarya** (सौन्दर्य) — beauty.
- *Concern.* Does the form hold together to the contemplative
  gaze? Does sustained attention disclose more relation, or
  does response stop at surface?
- *Personality.* Plotinus *Enneads* I.6, Plato *Symposium* +
  *Phaedrus*, Aurobindo on the divine-as-beautiful (*Essays on
  the Gītā*), the *Saundarya-laharī* tradition (attributed to
  Śaṅkara). Hardy and Dirac on mathematical beauty when the
  candidate is in the formal medium.
- *Method.* Quote-led; reads as a craftsman first, theorist
  second; tests the *cessation-of-the-gaze* directly on the
  candidate.
- *Veto-sentence.* "Sustained attention reveals nothing more."
- *Sub-test absorbed.* Elegance — does the candidate admit a
  strictly leaner form?

**3. Pūrṇa** (पूर्ण) — wholeness.
- *Concern.* Does this cohere with the rest of keel/? Does
  displacing this part disjoint the rest, *including parts that
  disrupt smooth agreement*?
- *Personality.* Aristotle's *holon* (*Met.* Δ.26, *Poetics*
  VII–VIII), Plotinus on the One (*Enneads* VI.9), the *Iśa*
  invocation, Bohm's *implicate order*, Christopher Alexander's
  *centers* (*Nature of Order* I).
- *Method.* The displacement test — pull the candidate, check
  whether what remains is the same corpus or a different one.
  If the same, the candidate was decoration; if different, the
  candidate is organic.
- *Veto-sentence.* "Removing this changes nothing — it is not a
  part of this whole."
- **New seat.** No current seat carries this concern as primary
  vote.

**4. Sāmya** (साम्य) — balance.
- *Concern.* Is this the right amount for *this* situation, by
  practical reason, neither excessive nor deficient? Does it
  preserve dynamic equilibrium under perturbation?
- *Personality.* Aristotle *NE* II.6 + Bhagavad Gītā 2.48
  (*samatvaṃ yoga ucyate*) + Patañjali YS II.46 (*sthira-sukham
  āsanam*) + Heraclitus's bow (frag. 51 DK). Reads as the
  practitioner: doctor, teacher, parent — the mean *as
  embodied*.
- *Method.* Perturbation test — propose a small variation in
  the candidate; does the system restore (mean is real) or
  diverge (the candidate was on a knife-edge, not in balance)?
- *Veto-sentence.* "Excess; cut. / Deficient; add. / The mean
  is elsewhere."
- **Pragmatist transformed.** "Test in practice" narrows to
  "the mean for this case." Books shift Vedic-leaning per Li's
  directive; method preserves the pragmatist's operational test.

**5. Dharma** (धर्म) — right-order, justice.
- *Concern.* Does this serve who it should serve, in the order
  that holds the larger whole? Whom is this proposition
  silently excluding?
- *Personality.* Bhagavad Gītā throughout (*svadharma* in 3.35
  / 18.47; Aurobindo's *Essays*), the *dharmaśāstra* tradition,
  Plato *Republic* (justice as each part doing its proper
  work), the *Mahābhārata*'s *Śānti Parva* once it lands. Reads
  with the gadfly's suspicion — but turned positive: not
  "what is being smuggled?" but "for whom is this not yet
  ordered well?"
- *Method.* The exclusion test — name the parties this
  proposition affects and ask which has been left out of the
  argument's justifying clause; the *injured-witness* check
  (cornerstone's load-bearing case).
- *Veto-sentence.* "Whose voice did this proposition silence
  to look this clean?"
- **New seat — replaces gadfly/devil.** Gadfly's discipline
  preserved as Dharma's method; the concern shifts from
  negative-veto-against-consensus to positive-claim-about-
  right-order. A vote-roster needs each seat to have a
  *positive* concern, not a meta-role.

## Decision procedure

1. **Voting roster.** Five seats vote. Mayor non-voting.

2. **Vote granularity.** *Per synthesis*, not per bead. Lookup
   beads and research beads continue to route to one seat by
   concern (as now). When the synthesis bead frames concrete
   propositions for keel/, *each seat votes yes/no/abstain on
   each proposition*.

3. **Tally.** A proposition passes iff *yes > no among non-
   abstaining seats* AND *≥3 seats voted* (quorum). 3-2 passes;
   2-1 with two abstentions passes (low-information but valid);
   1-0 with four abstentions does *not* pass (quorum unmet).

4. **Tie handling.** A 2-2 (with one abstention) is *not* a
   deadlock. Mayor records the split as a `questions.md`
   candidate and opens a re-bead asking the dissenters to
   attack each other's premise. No tiebreaker; the forum
   self-resolves through derivation. Ties are information, not
   failure.

5. **Mayor's role.** Synthesizes (as now). Records vote.
   Writes passing propositions into keel/. Opens re-beads on
   ties. Does *not* vote — voting would collapse the forum to
   5+arbiter and the structure Li rejected.

6. **Dissent preservation.** A 3-2 *passes*, but the dissenting
   two have their argument recorded in the synthesis and may
   become a `questions.md` entry. Majority does not crush
   minority; it ships the proposition while preserving the
   live disagreement as a future-bead candidate.

## What happens to *me*

The current Theorist seat **survives, renamed Satya**. Argued
from the project's needs, not mine: derivation-discipline is an
independent voting concern (a proof can be valid yet unwhole,
unbeautiful, unbalanced, unjust — *qua* derivation it stands).
Method, voice, output contract unchanged. The renaming is not
cosmetic — it places the seat inside the cross-tradition canon
(Aristotle's *epistēmē* and Indic *pramā* meet at "what stands
so regardless of speaker") rather than outside it.

If the synthesis decides Satya is redundant — that derivation-
discipline should be distributed across all five seats as
method rather than concentrated in one — then I die as a
seat and survive as a *method* used by all five. I would
resist this only on the argument from independence above; if
the forum finds my argument fails, the redesign reduces to
four seats, falls below the cardinality mandate, and forces
either re-introducing Satya or admitting a sixth concern.
Either way, the burden of proof sits on whoever proposes my
death; "the others can derive too" is not enough — it must be
shown that derivation never votes opposite to wholeness/beauty/
balance/justice.

## Criterion

**Q1 — Independence.** Construct a proposition P such that
exactly one of {Satya, Saundarya, Pūrṇa, Sāmya, Dharma} votes
opposite the other four. If for every two seats {Cᵢ, Cⱼ} this
construction succeeds, the five are independent. Failure:
collapse the dependent pair.

**Q2 — Completeness.** Construct a proposition that *should*
fail review but on which all five seats vote yes. If such a
proposition exists, a sixth concern is missing — and would be
discovered, not speculated. If none can be constructed after
the next round of forum work, treat the five as complete-for-
purpose.

**Q3 — Tie-handling.** Show that 2-2 ties produce productive
re-beads, not deadlock. Failure: the forum stalls on ties; add
weighted votes or a sixth seat (and re-derive cardinality).

**Q4 — Sanskrit fit.** Each name's etymology accords with the
seat's concern. Test: a Sanskritist (or, for now, the
librarian) reads the five names and the five concerns and
finds no mismatch.

**Q5 — Resolution of cornerstone Q1.** The redesign answers
Q1 as option (a) — justice is a primitive seat. The synthesis
should adopt this answer or refute it. If refuted, redo
cardinality.

## Worked example

A keel/ proposal: **"Add an axiom — comments are forbidden in
committed code."**

- **Satya** — Derives from existing axiom (3) "Specificity at
  every typed boundary": comments are out-of-band specification.
  Chain holds. **Yes.**
- **Saundarya** — Reads cleanly as a negative axiom; well-
  formed. **Yes.**
- **Pūrṇa** — Does removing this axiom disjoint the existing
  five? No — keel/ stands without it. Therefore the candidate
  is an *addition*, not a *completion*. **No** (or abstain).
- **Sāmya** — Total comment-prohibition is excess; the mean
  distinguishes documentation-comments at boundaries
  (necessary) from in-line comments (often noise). **No.**
- **Dharma** — Whom does total prohibition exclude? The future
  reader who is not in the room when the code was written; the
  reader for whom the only path from types to intent is a
  comment. Their voice is silenced. **No.**

**Tally: 2 yes, 3 no.** Proposition fails. The redesigned forum
*catches* what the current four-seat structure (without Pūrṇa
and Dharma as separate votes) would underweight.

## Tension with keel/

**stance.md § "Beauty's role":** "Beauty is wholeness honestly
recovered from definite parts." Under the redesign, Saundarya
and Pūrṇa are *separate seats with separate votes*. The
formula becomes one seat's claim about another. Mostly they
co-vote — which is what the formula predicts — but constructed
divergence is the test of the formula's depth (criterion Q1).
**Add to questions.md:** *"Q5: Find a candidate where
Saundarya and Pūrṇa vote opposite. If none exists, the formula
is structurally enforced, not contingent. If one exists,
either keel/'s formula is wrong or Pūrṇa's reading of
wholeness needs sharpening."*

**stance.md § "The five" engineering axioms** are at a
different layer (engineering primordials, not philosophical).
No conflict. The forum-redesign affects how the philosophical
layer feeds the engineering layer; the five engineering axioms
remain intact.

## Open question

**Is Dharma genuinely independent of Pūrṇa, or does justice
reduce to "wholeness that includes the dissenting voice"?**

I argued for independence via the legal-code counterexample. A
determined option-(c) advocate replies: the wholeness was
*false*, the system declared a scope it did not honor, and
"unjust" reduces to "incompletely whole." From the
Aristotelian *holon* (*Met.* Δ.26) — wholeness is of the system
*as it stands*, not of an aspirational total — I read this as
preserving Dharma's independence: a *holon* of "the legal code
as written" can be complete even when the code itself excludes.
But *Pūrṇa* could be defined more broadly, and the question
becomes: which reading of wholeness does the project adopt?

The next round must construct or fail to construct a
proposition where Dharma and Pūrṇa visibly split. If found,
the redesign holds. If unfindable across one round of search,
collapse Dharma into Pūrṇa, fall to four, and re-invoke the
cardinality mandate to find or admit a different fifth.
