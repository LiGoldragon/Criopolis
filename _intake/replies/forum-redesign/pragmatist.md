**Position**

A four-seat forum is asked, "Should `keel/boundaries.md` add an `ephemeral` upgrade topology now?" Theorist and aesthete can say yes: the distinction is clean, the taxonomy feels whole. Pragmatist and gadfly can say no: there is no durable boundary yet, and the fifth kind may be count worship. At 2-2, the mayor either breaks the tie or synthesizes around it; either way, the mayor has become an unnamed fifth seat. Ship the fifth seat openly. My proposal is a five-seat forum: **Satya**, **Dharma**, **Artha**, **Rasa**, **Moksha**. Three is decisive but too compressed for this project's mix of engineering rules, metaphysical commitments, and bead-routing choices. Five is the smallest odd forum that lets truth, right order, means, felt value, and freedom from bondage each vote once without hiding inside another seat. Elegance dies as a primordial; it survives as a criterion inside Rasa constrained by Artha and Satya. Wholeness and balance move from seats to synthesis/protocol: they are what a good decision achieves, not extra voters.

**Source bones**

`library/en/bhagavad-gita/buitenen-bhagavad-gita-mahabharata.pdf` gives the operational frame: Arjuna's crisis is not an abstract request for ontology but a demand for a determinate course of action while his mind is "confused over what is the Law" (Gita 2). The same text insists on action without ownership of fruit and names yoga as skill in acts (2.47-50). That matters here: a forum that does not bind the next move is commentary, not decision.

`library/en/sri-aurobindo/essays-on-the-gita.pdf` is the best local bridge for the Vedic-rooted redesign. Aurobindo glosses dharma as that which one holds to and that which holds things together: law, norm, rule of nature, action, and life. He also reads the Gita as a synthesis of Knowledge, Works, and Love rather than as one school defeating the others. That argues against a single master seat and against collapsing the practical into the theoretical. It also justifies a forum where seats are powers of action, not ornamental virtues.

`library/en/upanisads/olivelle-early-upanisads.pdf` supplies two checks. Kena closes with truth as the abode of the hidden teaching; Isa says knowledge and ignorance must be known together, and that seeing beings in the self removes bewilderment and sorrow. For the forum, this means Satya cannot be demoted to taste or usefulness, and Moksha cannot be treated as anti-action. The release lens asks whether the decision frees the next action from delusion, attachment, or false framing. `library/en/sankara/vivekacudamani-madhavananda.pdf` makes the same operationally sharper: discrimination between the real and unreal and yearning for freedom are prerequisites, not decorative afterthoughts. This is a tradition-level use of the purushartha pattern; a primary dharmashastra anchor for that scheme should be added by the librarian if the mayor wants the final doctrine to cite adjudication texts directly.

**Cost ledger / decision**

The cost is one more reply than the current four-seat forum, one more personality to maintain, and more synthesis burden for the mayor. The payoff is concrete: no 2-2 deadlock, no hidden mayor vote, no duplicated beauty/elegance vote, and less chance that "pragmatic" swallows every other concern. The overlap risk is real, so the seats need hard refusal boundaries:

| Seat | Concern and method | Current-seat fate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Satya** | Holds the line on what is actually supported. Opens by stating the claim, source basis, counterexample, and falsifier. Votes no when the evidence or concept does not hold. | Theorist partially survives here, but loses ownership of all structure. |
| **Dharma** | Holds the line on right order: obligation, justice, role, harm, and whether the decision can rightly bind others. Opens by naming who pays and what rule of action is being created. | New seat; it absorbs the missing justice question without making justice a sixth free-floating axiom. |
| **Artha** | Holds the line on means: build cost, maintenance tail, runtime cost, blast radius, operational failure modes. Opens with a concrete scenario and a cost ledger. | Pragmatist survives here, narrower and cleaner. It no longer claims to be the whole cash-value test for every seat. |
| **Rasa** | Holds the line on felt value: beauty, desire, readability, livability, and whether the work is worth inhabiting. Opens with the experience of a reader or user under sustained contact. | Aesthete survives here; elegance becomes one of its tests, not its own throne. |
| **Moksha** | Holds the line on release: non-attachment to the current frame, reversibility, escape from lock-in, and whether the question should be refused or reframed. Opens by naming the bondage the proposal may create. | Gadfly/devil transforms here. The role is not contrarianism; it is freedom from false necessity. |

Decision procedure: the mayor frames a proposition as a yes/no next move with owner and scope. Each seat gets one unweighted vote: **yes**, **no**, or **reframe**. Reframe counts as no on the current proposition and must include a replacement proposition. Three matching votes bind the next move. The mayor does not vote and does not break ties; with five full votes there is no tie. The mayor may reject only for protocol failure: missing quorum, non-binary proposition, or a vote whose stated reason falls outside the seat's concern. Some questions do not admit yes/no; those are mayor-framing failures, not forum failures.

**Worked example**

Question: "Add `ephemeral` as a fifth upgrade topology in `keel/boundaries.md` today." In the current four-seat forum, the result plausibly deadlocks: aesthete yes for taxonomy, theorist yes for distinction, pragmatist no for ceremony cost, gadfly no for abstraction hunger. Mayor breaks the tie and the vote becomes theatre.

In the proposed forum: Satya says no because no concrete boundary has forced the distinction yet; Dharma says no because `keel/process.md` already says rules do not skip the report stage; Artha says no because the added branch taxes every boundary review; Rasa may say yes because the word clarifies an intuition, but admits that intuition is not enough; Moksha says no because naming it now binds future work to an untested frame. Result: 4-1 no. Binding next move: keep Q7 in `keel/questions.md`, wait for the first real running-process-only boundary, then decide. This changes conduct tomorrow.

**Tension with `keel/`**

`keel/README.md` currently names five engineering axioms and a four-seat forum. Do not map my five seats onto the five axioms. The axioms are rules for Rust and sema boundaries; the seats are a decision protocol for contested judgments. Confusing them would make Sanskrit names look profound while doing no engineering work.

The biggest tension is Rasa. `keel/stance.md` says beauty is diagnostic, not an acceptance gate; Rasa must obey that. If Rasa votes no, it must reduce the discomfort to a source, user, or wholeness failure that another seat can inspect. The second tension is Artha: the pragmatist's local-ugliness allowance must not become a license to reopen utility crates everywhere. Artha can say "ship the 200-line private CLI" only when the ugliness is local, named, and cheap to delete.

**Open question**

Is **Moksha** a permanent fifth seat, or should it become a rotating **Stranger** after the first three shape-setting votes? Ship Moksha for the trial because the project needs a freedom-from-frame vote; replace it if its reasons repeatedly duplicate Satya or Dharma.
