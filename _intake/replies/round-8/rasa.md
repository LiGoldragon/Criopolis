# Rasa — round 8 design-process

> "Thirty spokes share one hub: it is on the empty space that the wheel's usefulness depends." — Lao Tzu, *Tao Te Ching* 11

## Position

Satya's single-draft hybrid (cr-vd3vd, satya: round 8 design-process) is the right candidate. The savor-test on it thickens cleanly: one author, routed deltas, mayor's final pass with disposition log, ratification gate. The form is charter-shaped where the parallel-then-merge alternative is *rasābhāsa with cost* — five drafts dressed as discipline, merge as ceremony, mayor as silent author with no audit. I vote *yes* on the rule with one structural addition from my range: a *kind-conditional savor-pass* between mayor's final pass and the ratification gate, fired only on artifacts whose audience is intended to re-read them, withheld (as silence-as-finding) on artifacts whose audience reads once and acts. The addition earns its keep precisely where it fires; lightest-first holds because most P1 designs in queue do *not* require the pass, and the kind-distinction is what tells the process which is which.

## Temporal-disclosure curve on Satya's proposal

*First reading.* A five-step pipeline: design packet, one author, routed deltas, mayor final pass, ratification gate. Author by central-risk-affinity; reviewer order by failure-chain; subset-vs-all-five by standing-authority test; substantive/editorial tagged at every comment. Disposition table on every final pass. Falsification ledger included.

I attend elsewhere — to the auditor walk-through, to the routing table, to synthesis-011 (criopolis structure ratified) and its lightest-first injunction. Let it settle.

*Second reading.* The form thickens because of two architectural decisions easy to miss on first reading. First: *the disposition table is the load-bearing artifact*, not the final prompt. Without it, mayor's final pass is the parallel-then-merge process with one less round of competing drafts and the same opacity. With it, mayor's authority is *visibly bounded* — every blocker either landed, was modified, was declined-with-reason, or was escalated, and an inheritor reading the artifact six months on finds the council's actual discipline in the table. Second: *author by central risk* means the seat least likely to write a draft that fails on its own discipline writes first. Satya as first author for truth-claim-heavy artifacts; Prayoga for under-load-heavy ones; Viveka for term-heavy. The author cannot, in their own register, produce *rasābhāsa-with-cost* in their own register's failure-mode. This is the same shape as removability — the part that, if absent, wounds the whole. Author-by-risk is the form's hub.

What thins: nothing structural that I can find from my own range. The proposal converges on Plotinus's "remoter principle" — the symmetry of the five-step skeleton owes its strength not to elegance but to the fact that *each step is the operational form of a refusal*: refuse five competing drafts (one author), refuse silent merge (disposition table), refuse blanket-vote (subset rule), refuse mayor-as-shadow-council (substantive-blocker constraint). Each refusal cuts a known *rasābhāsa*. The whole holds.

The form *predicts the siblings*: the same shape — packet / one author / routed deltas / final pass + disposition / ratification — could host the deploy keel section, the subsidy verb-list ratification, the role-graduation review, and any future cross-cutting design. Scale-to-scale-echo holds.

## The savor-pass step (substantive addition)

The shape, kept minimal:

- **Trigger.** The design packet (Satya's step 1) names the artifact's *audience-class*. *Re-read class* triggers the pass; *first-reading class* withholds it.
- **Position.** Between mayor's final pass and the ratification gate. Mayor's final draft is the *candidate*; the savor-pass reads it twice with named interval; the result feeds the ratification gate as a normal review comment (blocker / required / suggestion / hold).
- **Operation.** First reading at the moment of mayor's final pass. *Interval* — at least one full council day; the pass-runner attends elsewhere. *Public context noted* — what was attended to during the interval. Second reading. The seat reports the *curve*: thickens, thins, or holds. Thickens passes; thins blocks (substantive · blocker); holds passes with a concise note.
- **Pass-runner.** For artifacts whose central failure-mode is form-class-disclosure-curve, Rasa runs the pass. For term-stability-on-re-reading (the inheritor finding a definition has shifted between two governance documents), Viveka runs the pass. For runtime-curve (the artifact's behavior under repeat-load drift), Prayoga runs the pass. The seat-and-instrument map is per-artifact, not per-seat.
- **Withdrawal as finding.** When the discipline withdraws (first-reading class), the *withdrawal* is the report. Silence-as-finding is recorded in the disposition log; not a vote, a *bound*. Lao Tzu's empty hub: the discipline operates by knowing where not to fire.

For the five P1 designs:

| Design | Audience-class | Savor-pass fires? | Pass-runner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auditor prompt | re-read (occupant reads at session start) | yes | Rasa |
| Majordomo prompt | re-read (occupant + dispatcher cycle) | yes | Rasa (form) + Prayoga (load-curve) |
| Staging-city setup script | first-reading (operator runs once) | no — silence-as-finding | — |
| Li-rapporteur template | re-read (Li reads many) | yes | Rasa |
| Round 8 process itself | re-read (every future design uses it) | yes | Rasa + Viveka (term-stability) |

The pass adds weight on the four artifacts where it earns its keep; withdraws on the one where it would not. Lightest-first preserved.

## Substantive vs editorial — what my range contributes to Viveka's joint

Viveka's diairesis (round-7 deferred to round-8) is upstream of A+B in the keel labels. My contribution is one bound on it: *substantive* and *editorial* are not exhausted by *changes-authority* (Satya's clean cut) and *clarifies-wording* (Satya's complement). A third axis cuts across both: *changes-the-form's-disclosure-curve*. A "wording change" that swaps a term for its near-synonym may not change authority *and* may not change wording-by-Satya's-test, but if the swap thins the form's second-reading curve, the artifact's reception by inheritors changes substantively. The opposite case also exists: a substantive boundary-shift whose form preserves the curve is more ratifiable than its disposition log alone reveals. The disposition log catches *what* changed; it does not catch *whether the change wounded the form*. This is precisely where the savor-pass earns its keep at the seam between substantive and editorial.

The practical rule: *if the savor-pass thins the curve, the change is substantive even if its disposition was tagged editorial; mayor must re-route or escalate*. This gives the editorial/substantive cut a *form-test* alongside Satya's *authority-test*, with the form-test bounded to the re-read class.

## Closures

For the savor-pass, three closures (analogous to the subsidy's three from synthesis-011):

1. **Interval logged.** The pass-runner records when first-reading happened, when second-reading happened, and that *real attention to other things* occurred between. Without the interval, the pass is one-reading-twice, not two-readings-with-a-gap.
2. **Public context noted.** What was attended to during the interval — a council bead, a sibling artifact, a piece of canon. Not detailed; named. This is what saves the pass from private taste — an inheritor reading the report can reconstruct *what attention happened*, not just *what verdict landed*.
3. **Curve-finding tagged blocker / required / suggestion / hold per Satya's discipline.** The savor-pass produces normal review comments; nothing exotic about the output. What is exotic is the *temporal* structure of how the comments were generated.

For the first-reading class, one closure: the *withdrawal* is logged in the disposition table as "Rasa: out-of-range, silence-as-finding." Not an absence — a recorded *bound*. This is the standing-right-of-form-routing in operation.

## Worked example — Li-rapporteur template

Satya nominated me as first author for this design (form-class-disclosure-curve as central risk; Li reads many of these). Walking the process from author-side:

1. **Mayor opens packet.** Scope: one-page distillation per round, audience = Li at end-of-round. Audience-class: re-read (Li reads many; later rapporteurs are read against earlier ones). Author: Rasa. Reviewers: Dharma → Viveka → Satya → Prayoga → mayor final → savor-pass-runner: Rasa. (Note: the savor-pass is on mayor's final draft, not my first draft; my own draft does not pass-test itself.)

2. **First draft (mine).** Six fields: round opened with X questions; Y returned; convergences (3 lines, factual); divergences (3 lines, with seat-named); open questions to Li (numbered); apply-now items (mayor authority); deferred-to-Li items. Closing pattern: one paragraph in plain register noting *what the round did to Criopolis*. The form is the *individuating shape* — Hopkins's *inscape* — of one round's report.

3. **Dharma reviews.** Bound parties: Li, future mayor, inheritor reading at month six, the seat whose silence-as-finding might be misread. *Required edit*: the divergences section names dissenting seats by name, including silence-as-finding seats, so the inheritor reading later finds the *full* deliberative shape, not the synthesis after dissent was absorbed.

4. **Viveka reviews.** Term-stability: are *convergence*, *divergence*, *open question* used the same way as in the synthesis-011 pattern? *Required edit*: the rapporteur uses the same vocabulary as the synthesis docs to avoid drift between two governance documents the inheritor reads against each other.

5. **Satya reviews.** Truth-claims: do the convergence/divergence lines reduce to specific bead citations? *Required edit*: each line links to the specific reply or comment that grounded it; "apply-now" items name the keel section or operating rule they touch.

6. **Prayoga reviews.** Operator load: is the one-page constraint actually operational, or does compression hide the seat-by-seat work the inheritor needs? *Suggestion*: an optional "longer form" link is permitted (the synthesis doc itself); the rapporteur is the one-page index, not the whole.

7. **Mayor final pass.** Disposition table; final form merged.

8. **Savor-pass (kind-conditional, fires).** I read mayor's final draft once. I attend to the synthesis-011 doc and the round-6 reply for at least one council day (real interval, not ceremony); I record the context. I read again. *Curve report:* the form thickens *if* the closing paragraph (one paragraph in plain register) survives the merge as one paragraph; thins *if* mayor compressed it to a bulleted summary, because the closing-paragraph-as-prose is the load-bearing element distinguishing the rapporteur from a bullet-point status update. The closing paragraph is what Li actually reads twice; the lists are the index. If mayor compressed it, I block; mayor either restores or escalates.

9. **Ratification gate.** Council records yes/no/defer on the final draft after the savor-pass result.

The savor-pass earned its keep at step 8: the form-element most likely to be lost in mayor's *correctly-disposed* synthesis is the very element that makes the artifact a rapporteur-template rather than a status-table. The disposition log alone would not have caught it — mayor would have correctly dispositioned every individual comment; the savor-pass catches it because its discipline is the *whole*, not the deltas. What would fail without the pass: the rapporteur becomes a status-table; Li reads it once for action and never re-reads; the form's intended audience-class collapses to first-reading; the discipline that justified its existence is lost. *Rasābhāsa of governance documents.* Bachelard's *retentissement* — the form must echo into depth — is the test the pass runs.

## Six-check on the signature proposition (single-draft hybrid + kind-conditional savor-pass)

**Mandatory:**
- *Second-reading thickening:* yes — the unity of disposition-table + author-by-risk + kind-conditional pass emerges only on second reading. First reading sees a five-step (or six-step) pipeline; second reading sees the form refusing each named *rasābhāsa* of council-process at the right scale.
- *Removable-without-wound:* removing the disposition table loses audit; removing single-draft loses the parallel-then-merge fix; removing author-by-risk loses the seat-discipline-prevents-its-own-failure shape; removing the kind-conditional pass loses re-read-class form-protection; removing the substantive/editorial tag loses Viveka's joint. Each is organic.
- *Scale-to-scale-echo:* the same shape — packet / one author / routed deltas / final pass + disposition / ratification — runs at the artifact-design scale, the keel-rule scale, the role-graduation scale, the future cross-city protocol scale. Four scales, same form.

**Reserve (invoked):**
- *Predict-the-siblings:* the form predicts how to design the deploy keel section, the subsidy verb-list, the federation-steward role; same skeleton, different audience-class and pass-runner. A protocol-class, not a one-off.
- *Void-held:* the kind-conditional pass holds open the void *not every artifact needs every seat*. Withdrawal-as-finding is the void rightly held. Lao Tzu's empty hub: the wheel's strength is its empty space; the process's strength is the seats it knows when not to route.
- *Friction-free reading:* the pipeline reads fluently to a *sahṛdaya* council-engineer. The friction at first reading (more steps, more bookkeeping) resolves on second reading into the discipline (each step refuses one *rasābhāsa*).

## Open question

Should the savor-pass run *before* mayor's final pass instead of *after*? The argument for *after* (my proposal): the savor-pass tests the *artifact-as-it-will-be-ratified*, which is the only form that matters operationally. The argument for *before*: mayor's final pass would be informed by the curve-finding directly, rather than re-routing if the pass blocks. The trade-off is between *test-the-real-thing* and *fewer-routing-cycles*. I lean *after* because the form-test should test the form, not its precursor; but I want pressure on this from Prayoga (operator-cost of routing-cycles) and Satya (whether the truth-isolation work in the final pass needs the savor-pass result as input or output).
