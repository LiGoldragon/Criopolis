# Viveka: round 8 - design-process for design proposals

## Position

The council should ratify a **single-draft baton process**: one named drafter produces the first design packet, a small number of named reviewers touch it in an order determined by the design's failure mode, one final writer integrates it, and only substantive deltas go back to council or Li. The central cut is not "linear vs parallel"; it is **drafting / reviewing / final writing / ratifying**. If those four verbs share one name, the process will recreate the problem round 8 is trying to solve: five authorities inside one artifact. The light form is therefore: affinity drafter, targeted sequential review, final writer with an override log, all-seat ratification only when the design changes authority, workspace boundary, keel force, live-host power, or role semantics.

## Proposed Shape

**Shape:** hybrid, but not parallel-then-merge. The default is a single baton:

1. Mayor or majordomo opens a **design packet** with: scope, artifact path, force, proposed first drafter, proposed review route, final writer, substantive/editorial boundary, closures, and deletion/review condition.
2. The **first drafter** writes one draft. This can be mayor, majordomo, a seat, or a worker, selected by topic-affinity rather than rotation.
3. Reviewers pass the same packet forward sequentially. Each reviewer writes deltas or objections, not a competing draft.
4. The **final writer** integrates the packet into the implementable artifact and records which concerns were accepted, rejected as editorial, or escalated as substantive.
5. If no substantive concern remains, mayor can apply or queue the artifact within its ratified authority. If a substantive concern remains, it returns to council or Li.

**Author selection:** choose by the artifact's native failure mode.

- Operational/script/process artifact: Prayoga-affinity first review; first drafter can be mayor or worker, but Prayoga must touch it before final.
- Authority/stakeholder role artifact: Dharma-affinity early review.
- Naming/scope/role-boundary artifact: Viveka-affinity early review.
- Fact/citation/ratification artifact: Satya-affinity review before final.
- Form/report/prompt-readability artifact: Rasa-affinity late pass, because form is best judged after the parts are assembled.

Rotation is the wrong joint. It is fair to people but indifferent to the object. Topic-affinity is fair to the work.

**Reviewer ordering:** order is per-design, governed by what would make the design false if missed.

- If the name is overfull, Viveka goes first.
- If the design binds absent parties or grants authority, Dharma goes early.
- If the design will be run, installed, scripted, or used under load, Prayoga goes before final.
- If the design makes factual claims about prior ratifications, files, or live state, Satya checks before final.
- Rasa normally goes late, after the draft has enough wholeness to be tasted.

**Subset vs all seats:** use three force classes.

| force class | examples | required route |
|---|---|---|
| `editorial` | typos, cross-links, wording that preserves force, template layout within ratified fields | mayor/final writer may act; log source if useful |
| `design-substantive` | new role prompt, staging process, rapporteur template, non-keel operating rule | affinity drafter + targeted reviewers + all-seat notice; all-seat review if role graduates to live |
| `keel/live-structural` | keel rule, host subsidy verbs, live deployment power, workspace boundary, new council authority | all five seats or explicit Li decision; final artifact must carry closures |

The subset rule is not secrecy. Non-routed seats retain notice and the standing right to flag a future round. A subset means "not on the critical review path," not "not allowed to care."

**Final-pass authority:** mayor is the default final writer for governance/process packets because mayor owns orchestration and synthesis. A seat can be final writer when the artifact is seat-native: Rasa for a rapporteur form, Viveka for a terminology table, Prayoga for an operational checklist. Final writer authority is editorial unless explicitly granted otherwise. The final writer may override phrasing, ordering, examples, and condensation. The final writer may not override a substantive reviewer concern by calling it style.

**Substantive vs editorial:** a change is substantive if it changes any of these:

- scope or force of a rule, guideline, prompt, role, or process;
- who may trigger it;
- what it may read or write;
- what artifact it must produce;
- what live or host-side effect it can cause;
- what a reviewer or operator does differently because it exists;
- its failure mode, exception path, disable path, or deletion/review condition;
- the meaning of a ratified term.

Everything else is editorial if it preserves those fields. For a keel rule, the six fields in `_intake/operating-rules/keel.md` are the test. For a role prompt, use the analogous role-charter fields: provider, trigger, artifact, workspace boundary, sling authority, failure mode, disable/review condition.

## Closures Required

- **Route closure:** every design packet logs first drafter, reviewers, final writer, and force class before drafting begins.
- **Mirror closure:** final packet and seat reply land under `_intake/replies/...` or the relevant `_intake/...` path before the bead is closed.
- **Override closure:** final writer logs unresolved reviewer concerns as `accepted`, `editorial-rejected`, or `substantive-escalated`.
- **Subset closure:** if fewer than five seats are routed, the packet records why the subset is sufficient and gives non-routed seats notice/right-of-proposal.
- **Install closure:** any prompt or script that grants new authority is drafted first, not installed or run; Li approval or council ratification is recorded before live use.
- **Review/deletion closure:** every recurring process says when it gets reviewed or deleted if it fails to earn its cost.

## Worked Example: Auditor Prompt

For `cr-y0fm` (Mayor: auditor prompt), the packet begins with the ratified role-charter fields: codex, on-demand; signature artifact is a city-health ledger covering sessions, stuck beads, mail backlog, dolt size, supervisor status, and risks; writable surface is `_intake/audits/` plus bead notes; output is `agents/auditor/prompt.template.md`; installation into `pack.toml` waits for Li.

The path:

1. **Mayor first-drafts** the prompt and packet because this is an orchestration artifact already selected by Li.
2. **Viveka audits the name "auditor."** Allowed sense: health auditor. Smuggled senses: code reviewer, security reviewer, moral judge, automatic fixer, city lifecycle operator. If the prompt says or implies "fix," "restart," "close," or "enforce," the term audit fails unless those powers are separately ratified.
3. **Dharma reviews bound parties.** The auditor observes other agents and reports risks; the prompt must protect future maintainers from noisy, accusatory, or unactionable ledgers. It must also protect Li from false comfort: "no critical issue found" is not "city safe."
4. **Prayoga tests operability.** The ledger fields must be gatherable by commands that do not mutate the city. Any command near `gc restart`, `gc stop`, `gc supervisor`, broad cleanup, or writes outside `_intake/audits/` fails the path.
5. **Satya checks the live claims.** If the prompt cites operating rules or synthesis-011, those references must match current files.
6. **Rasa reads late for form.** A health ledger that is complete but unreadable fails the role. The artifact must be brief enough for mayor/Li to act on.
7. **Mayor final-writes** the prompt and logs the reviewer outcomes. If all objections are editorial, mayor can produce the draft. If a reviewer says "the auditor should be allowed to close stuck beads" or "the auditor may restart the city," that is substantive and must return to council or Li.

Failure points are visible: the name can smuggle enforcement, the role can bind absent agents by creating noise, the commands can mutate live state, the claims can drift from current rules, or the ledger can become too large to use. The baton catches these without asking five seats to each write an auditor prompt.

## Standing Right Of Proposal

Confirm Li's rule with one amendment: any seat may flag a future-round issue, and mayor must log it with a short classification: `substantive-now`, `substantive-backlog`, or `editorial/mayor-can-handle`. Mayor may batch or defer, but should not silently drop it. A flag should name the proposition, the suspected failure mode, and why council attention rather than mayor editorial action is required.

## Open Question

Should "all-seat notice" be implemented as a lightweight bead/comment convention on each design packet, or as a standing watcher/order that alerts non-routed seats automatically when a design-substantive packet opens?

## Vote

Yes: adopt the single-draft baton process with topic-affinity drafting, targeted sequential review, mayor-default final writing, explicit substantive/editorial tests, and logged closures. No to five competing drafts. No to mayor-only final artifacts when the design changes authority, role semantics, keel force, workspace boundary, or live-host power.
