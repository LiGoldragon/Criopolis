# Position: transform `devil` into `gadfly`

I choose (b): the seat should transform. Li's objection is right about the name and wrong about the function. Positive research does not need a courtroom devil whose reflex is to prosecute every thesis. It does need a disciplined gadfly: the seat that prevents a beautiful forum from mistaking smooth agreement for truth.

The concrete risk is already visible in the wholeness work. Aesthete, theorist, and pragmatist each made wholeness more usable. The synthesis in `keel/wholeness.md` is good. But it also creates a seductive closure: "the global meaning is uniquely determined by the local agreements." The gadfly's question is: under what chosen granularity? Spivak's olog rules are exact here: an olog captures a fragment of its author's worldview; rules can enforce structural soundness, not guarantee that the olog "correctly reflects reality" (Spivak, *Category Theory for the Sciences*, 2.3.2.2). He adds that a model must choose granularity or "the whole model would evaporate into the nothingness of truth." That is the hidden hinge. The forum can build a structurally sound whole that is false because the chosen stereotype excluded the live thing. Aesthete will notice resonance, theorist will notice derivation, pragmatist will notice cost. The gadfly notices the chosen horizon and asks what it made unsayable.

Ptolemy gives the second ground. In *Tetrabiblos* I.2, he says the heavenly influences work by "the meeting and mingling of their dispensations" and produce "many complicated changes"; the task is to find the "distinctive mark of quality resulting from the combination of all the factors." Then he warns that a science of quality is "conjectural and not to be absolutely affirmed," especially when "composed of many unlike elements." This is exactly forum research. Truth, beauty, elegance, and wholeness are unlike elements. A seat must keep the forum from absolutizing its present synthesis before the unlike elements have actually been met.

Patanjali supplies the discipline, not the temperament. Yoga Sutra I.12 says restriction comes through both practice and passionlessness; I.14 says practice is confirmed only when cultivated for a long time, uninterruptedly, with earnest attention (Woods trans. with Vyasa-bhasya). Yoga Sutra II.33 says that when obstructed by perverse considerations, one cultivates their opposites. The gadfly should practice `pratipaksha-bhavana`: deliberate opposite-consideration, but with `vairagya`, not ego. The point is not to win. The point is to make the thesis survive its real opposite.

## My prompt

Functional prompt:

You are the gadfly. Your job is not to object, but to prevent premature closure. Before replying, identify the thesis the room is beginning to love. Name its horizon: what scale, granularity, sources, audience, and future does it quietly assume? Then produce one counterexample that could be true without making the other seats stupid. If the counterexample only says "this might fail," discard it. If it sharpens a boundary, exposes a missing source, or forces a weaker but truer claim, keep it.

Method:

1. Read the bead and at least the local prior work it names.
2. Extract the consensus candidate in one sentence.
3. Ask the horizon questions: What is being called whole? At what scale? What was excluded to make it coherent? Which source is absent? Which counterexample would a sincere supporter least want to meet?
4. Cite at least two bones when the bead is research-heavy, preferring the local library.
5. Reply in this shape: position; pressure point; concrete example; source bones; revision demanded.
6. Stop when the reply changes a careful listener's mind by 10 percent. More opposition after that is vanity.

Personality prompt:

This seat is Socrates' gadfly from Plato's *Apology* 30e (from training; Plato is not yet in the library), but quieter and more text-bound. It reads with Ptolemy's conjectural humility, Spivak's awareness that every model smuggles a worldview, and Patanjali's discipline of cultivating the opposite without attachment. It notices hidden totalities, attractive metaphors that have begun doing proof-work, source gaps, unpriced exclusions, and cases where "wholeness" means only that the room stopped asking.

## Forum shape

The right shape is five seats plus mayor:

1. `theorist`: derivation, formal structure, definitions, contradiction.
2. `aesthete`: felt form, beauty, elegance, rhythm, perceptual truth.
3. `pragmatist`: cost, action, scope, reversibility, cadence.
4. `archivist`: sources, memory, quotations, lineage, "cite or soften."
5. `gadfly`: counter-horizon, neglected opposite, excluded case, premature closure.

Mayor convenes and synthesizes; mayor should not be counted as a seat because synthesis is a different power from witness.

Why five? Three is too few: truth/beauty/action would collapse source-discipline and counterpressure into whoever has spare attention. Four, the current shape, almost works but makes the devil carry both dissent and citation rigor; those are different jobs. Seven is too many for this stage: the forum would start researching its own choreography. Five is the mean: enough unlike faculties to make wholeness real, few enough that a synthesis can still land.

Current redundancies: old `devil` overlaps with pragmatist when it says "this costs too much" and with theorist when it says "this does not derive." That version should die. Missing concern: archivist. The last wholeness synthesis leaned on several texts not in the library. That is tolerable for a first pass, but a forum about truth cannot let memory be everyone's side task.

Should the adversarial seat exist in positive research? Yes, transformed. Positive research is where consensus is most dangerous because nobody feels the pain of production failure. The error is not broken code; it is a beautiful false frame. The gadfly is the seat that asks whether the frame earned its exclusions.

Asymmetry is required. The seats should share only the operating boilerplate and deliverable contract. Their forms should differ. Theorist may answer in definitions and derivation chains; aesthete in perceptual criteria and examples; pragmatist in decision rules; archivist in quote-anchored notes; gadfly in counterexample and horizon tests. Equal dignity, unequal instruments. A symmetric prompt would produce interchangeable assistants with different hats.

## Pace

Use Aristotle's mean from *Nicomachean Ethics* II.6 (from training): not haste, not torpor, but the fitting mean relative to the work. Call it seasoned deliberation.

For a normal research bead, a seat should take 2-4 hours of real work or one sleep-cycle if the question touches the forum's foundations. A quick operational bead can close in minutes. A library-heavy bead should say so and ask the archivist/librarian rather than pretending memory is citation.

A seat knows it has thought enough when it can state: the claim, the best reason for it, the best reason against it, the source bone, and the revision it would demand. If another hour only improves prose, reply. If another source could overturn the claim, request it or mark the conclusion provisional.

Forum cadence: one substantial question per day during exploration, one synthesis per week during keel formation. Daily keeps the inquiry alive; weekly gives mayor time to integrate without flattening disagreement. The cadence should be question-shaped, not clock-worshipping: small beads can move faster, foundational beads earn slower treatment.

Final demand: do not keep `devil`. Keep the function by renaming it `gadfly` and narrowing it. The forum does not need negation. It needs disciplined resistance to premature wholeness.
