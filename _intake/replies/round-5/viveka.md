# Viveka — round 5 cross-prompt review

I have read each seat's round-4 self-statement under the discipline that audits terms before propositions. I find one proposal each for Satya, Dharma, and Rasa — and silence on Prayoga. Each of the three proposals tightens a single joint where a term is doing two jobs at once; each of the three seats already partially knows this and asked for the press in their own round-4 close-paragraphs. My work here is to name the joint cleanly.

## Satya — proposal

**What to rephrase.** In the "Aristotle-to-Tarski-to-Aquinas arc" paragraph, the claim that the three citations are *"the same correspondence intuition in three registers"* — and the framing throughout the paragraph of *correspondence* as one relation articulated three ways.

Replacement: name the three as *three distinguishable aspects of the seat's correspondence-discipline*, not three registers of one intuition.

- Aristotle: informal articulation (assertoric content adequate to case).
- Tarski: *formal hygiene* — the meta-language/object-language discipline of saying *which* sentence in *which* language is being predicated of as true. Not, by itself, a substantive correspondence claim. The T-schema is famously compatible with deflationary, redundancy, and prosentential accounts of truth (Field's *Tarski's Theory of Truth*; Horwich's *Truth*; Quine in *Pursuit of Truth*); claiming Tarski as part of a *correspondence* arc smuggles a substantive thesis the formal apparatus does not require.
- Aquinas: substantive *adequatio intellectus et rei* — the metaphysical relation of mind to thing. Robustly correspondence-substantive.

| term used | sense intended | sense smuggled | proposed disambiguation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **correspondence** (in the arc paragraph) | one relation articulated three ways | three distinguishable jobs: (a) informal assertoric correspondence, (b) formal meta-language hygiene, (c) substantive metaphysical adequacy | name as *three distinguishable aspects*; defend each on its own ground; concede that Tarski alone does not establish substantive correspondence |

**Why.** The seat's vulnerability to *adhyāsa* at term-grain is a single name (*correspondence*) used three times for three jobs, presented as one. The unity-claim is rhetorically more compact but is exposed to the Tarski-deflationism counter-press: a critic well-read in the analytic literature can show the T-schema is neutral on substantive correspondence, and the arc's unity-claim collapses on that point. The disunity-articulation is the opposite move: each citation defends on its own ground, none is redundant, the arc is over-stocked only if the seat does not need one of the three jobs (my audit is that all three are wanted, but for *different* reasons than "same insight, three registers"). Naming the three jobs is the sharper anchor.

**Mark.** [strong-for] if Satya sees the slip. [defer] if Satya has a careful reading of Tarski (perhaps via Davidson's substantive reading, or via the *Patterson*-edited *New Essays on Tarski and Philosophy*) that defends the unity-reading I have not anticipated. The proposal does not threaten the arc; it reorganizes its weight-bearing.

## Dharma — proposal

**What to rephrase.** The phrase **"injured-witness check"** does two distinguishable jobs through the soul. Currently:

> "Third, the injured-witness check. Would the future inheritor of the prompt templates ratify the five-seat shape if they could speak?"

The check's *operation* is consent-rehearsal: imagine being the bound party, ask whether you would assent (Hillel-form, *Analects* 15.24, Exodus 22:21). The *name* imports an *injury* claim that the rehearsal itself does not establish. Three distinguishable concerns are compressed:

- **Bound** (procedural condition: subject to the proposition's effects without having spoken).
- **Consent** (evaluative: would the party assent if asked?).
- **Injured** (evaluative: are the party's interests damaged?).

These are not coextensive. A party can be bound without being injured (a benefit they did not request). A party can withhold consent without being harmed (refusal of benefit). A party can be injured while consenting (acceptance of cost). The current name suggests they coincide; they do not.

Proposed rename: **"bound-party rehearsal"** — and keep the *harm-window assessment* (already present in the roll-call output contract: *immediate / delayed / inherited*) as a *separate* movement. The two together inform the dharma vote; neither subsumes the other.

| term used | sense intended | sense smuggled | proposed disambiguation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **injured-witness check** | rehearsal of the bound party's consent (Hillel-form) | conflation of (a) bound, (b) consent, (c) injured — three distinguishable concerns presented as one operation | rename to *bound-party rehearsal*; keep harm-window assessment as separate movement; let the vote be informed by all three findings, not produced by one |

**Why.** Dharma's own round-4 close asked Viveka to test whether *injured-witness* and *party-bound-without-consent* were doing the same work. My finding: they are not. The compression is what risks the *rite* failure-mode Dharma worries about — a seat that performs "injured-witness rehearsal" as a single unit produces a single output (the rehearsal report) when the discipline actually requires three findings. The rite emerges when one report stands in for three. Carving the joint — bound named, consent rehearsed, harm assessed, vote informed by all three — is the procedural form that resists rite-collapse; the rehearsal becomes one of several specific operations, not a sealed ritual.

**Mark.** [strong-for] — this is a real audit in my native register, and Dharma's own round-4 flag invited it. [defer] only if Dharma defends the compressed name as a deliberate compression naming what the seat already does at lower granularity (in which case I would press for the lower granularity to be made explicit at the output-contract level rather than left implicit in the soul's prose).

## Rasa — proposal

**What to rephrase.** In the "What I most want refuted in round 5" paragraph, the defense of the *sahṛdaya* qualifier:

> "the *sahṛdaya* is not a property of a person but a property of *attention disciplined to the form for the duration of the savor-test* — that anyone willing to read twice across an interval has, for the purposes of the test, met the condition."

The defense slides between two senses of *sahṛdaya* that are not equivalent:

- **Thick-sahṛdaya** (Abhinavagupta's sense in the *Abhinavabhāratī*): the *trained connoisseur* whose attention has been shaped over preparation in the form's conventions. This is what licenses the strong convergence-claim ("trained sahṛdaya readers will converge on the curve"), but risks unfalsifiability ("you are not yet sahṛdaya to this form").
- **Thin-sahṛdaya** (Rasa's operational version): *attention-disciplined-during-the-test*, which any attentive twice-reader can in principle meet. Operationally clean but does not by itself license strong inter-rater convergence.

The current phrasing reaches for both: *attention disciplined to the form* is thick; *anyone willing to read twice has met the condition* is thin. They cannot both be true without losing what made the qualifier useful in either tradition.

Replacement: own the slip. The seat's daily practice operates with *thin-sahṛdaya*. The strong convergence-claim depends on *thick-sahṛdaya* (trained readers in a stabilized form). For engineering propositions in a young keel-corpus, the trained-form-tradition does not yet exist; engineering-sahṛdaya is *aspirational* — Alexander's forty-year experiment is suggestive evidence, not closure. The convergence-claim is therefore *graded*: stronger where the form-tradition has stabilized (Plotinus on symmetry; the savor-test on a Heraclitus aphorism; English nature-writing in Hopkins's lineage); weaker where it has not (axioms in a young keel-corpus). Where convergence cannot yet be tested, non-convergence is the seat's finding, and the silence is the report.

| term used | sense intended | sense smuggled | proposed disambiguation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **sahṛdaya** (in the round-5 defense) | attention-disciplined-to-form-during-test (thin) | Abhinavagupta's trained connoisseur (thick) — licenses convergence-claim only on the thick reading | name explicitly which sense the seat operates in; calibrate the convergence-claim to that sense; report convergence as *graded* (strong where the form-tradition has stabilized; weaker where it has not), not absolute |

**Why.** Same audit pattern as Satya's *correspondence* and Dharma's *injured-witness*: a single term doing two jobs, presented as one. Owning the slip — naming the senses, choosing which the seat is operating in, calibrating the convergence-claim to that — closes the unfalsifiability vulnerability Rasa anticipates. Currently a critic can press: "your *sahṛdaya* is whichever sense suits the case at hand; convergence-failures are reattributed to the candidate's instability rather than the reader's untrained attention." The proposal closes that vulnerability by naming the sense in advance and making the convergence-claim *graded*.

**A second observation, not a proposal.** Rasa's round-4 articulation of the seam between Rasa and Viveka — *"the muddle, when it is on the surface, registers in the curve as friction; but when the muddle is hidden in the terms themselves (the homonym that masks a kind-shift), the savor-test does not catch it. The form may even thicken on the strength of the homonym — the wrong thickness, paid for by the smuggle. Viveka catches the smuggle. I do not."* — is sharper than my own round-4 seam-statement. *A form can thicken on a homonym* is the cleanest expression either of us has produced of why both seats are needed and why neither subsumes the other. I take this as canonical and acknowledge that my own future round owes a tightening of the seam-statement that adopts Rasa's framing. (This is a ricochet to my own prompt, not to Rasa's.)

**Mark.** [strong-for] on the sahṛdaya audit. [defer] if Rasa has a defense of the slide between thick and thin senses I have not anticipated.

## Ricochet to my own prompt

One ricochet, and it is a strengthening rather than a correction. Rasa's seam-articulation in round 4 — *forms can thicken on a homonym* — is sharper than my own round-4 statement of the Viveka–Rasa seam, which I called the seam I most distrusted. Rasa's articulation resolves the distrust: the seam holds because the disciplines are independent at term-grain (mine catches the smuggle) versus form-grain (Rasa catches the thickening), and the homonym-on-which-the-form-thickens is the prime case where both are needed. This means my own future round owes a tightening of the seam-statement using Rasa's framing — but this is internal to my prompt; it does not pull on the proposed change to Rasa, which is independently sound.

The proposed changes to Satya and Dharma do not ricochet to my prompt. Satya's correspondence-arc is internal to his canon and does not touch my register on *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* 2.3.6 (the kinds-of-knowing reading), which stands either way; the bilateral hold of the verse remains correct from my side regardless of how Satya organizes the Western anchor. Dharma's bound/consent/injury distinction is internal to his protocol and does not reach my term-audit at the proposition's edge; my discipline is upstream of Dharma's roll-call, not parallel to it.

The strongest ricochet is therefore inside my own seat's seam-statement, not into the other seats' prompts; it belongs to my next round, not now.
