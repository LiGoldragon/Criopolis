# Round 5 — Rasa cross-prompt review

> "A thing whose presence or absence makes no visible difference is not an organic part of the whole." — Aristotle, *Poetics* 8

The savor-test on four prompts: read once, attend elsewhere, read again. Two proposals, two silences. The discipline below names what thickens, what thins, and what the soul announces but the form does not yet embody.

## Satya — proposal

**What to add.** Insert a required field in the output contract for the **falsification ledger**, mirroring how the soul names it.

Current contract:
```
- **Position** (one paragraph)
- **Source bones** (cite library where possible; mark *(paraphrase from memory; flag for librarian)* otherwise)
- **Worked example** (where applicable)
- **Open question** (one)
- **Vote**: yes / no / defer — and tag the proposition's claim-type (`fact / definition / frame / rule / recommendation / experiment`) so the next-action distinguishes by type.
```

Proposed:
```
- **Position** (one paragraph)
- **Source bones** (cite library where possible; mark *(paraphrase from memory; flag for librarian)* otherwise)
- **Falsification ledger** (where the proposition admits truth-functional vote): three to five items naming what would refute the claim if observed, each marked *decisive / evidential / heuristic*. Silence-as-finding clause: if no truth-claim at proposition-grain, write "no ledger; defer."
- **Worked example** (where applicable)
- **Open question** (one)
- **Vote**: yes / no / defer — and tag the proposition's claim-type (`fact / definition / frame / rule / recommendation / experiment`) so the next-action distinguishes by type.
```

**Why, from my discipline.** Satya's soul names the ledger as the seat's *vow*: "the ledger is *written*, not held in mind. Every truth-vote the seat casts comes with an explicit list — usually three to five items — of what would refute the claim if observed. The list is part of the vote. Without it, the vote is a private gesture; with it, the vote is a *commitment*." The soul also names the gradation (decisive / evidential / heuristic). The contract enforces neither.

This is a form-and-substrate mismatch the savor-test catches structurally. Compare across seats:

- Viveka's contract requires the **terminology audit table** (signature, structural).
- Dharma's contract requires the **roll-call** with harm-window per party (signature, structural).
- Prayoga's contract requires the **application ledger**: decision-changes / cost-cohort / failure-mode-shifts (signature, structural).
- Mine requires the **temporal-disclosure curve** and **six-check** (signature, structural).
- Satya's contract requires the claim-type tag at the vote, but neither truth-isolation restatement nor falsification ledger as structural fields.

A seat that announces a signature pair as its vow but does not enforce the artifact in the contract is in the form-condition my soul names *rasābhāsa with discipline*: the form of holding-together at the level of the soul's prose, with the contract disclosing — under sustained attention — that nothing is structurally held. The ledger as soul-prescription without contract-enforcement is exactly the move Satya's own falsification-discipline would catch in another seat. Make the contract say what the soul says.

The "where the proposition admits truth-functional vote" qualifier preserves Satya's silence-as-finding clause: a bead carrying a frame-proposal, an aesthetic question, an operational rule, or an ethical stake does not require the ledger, and the absence is the seat's report.

**Mark: [strong-for].** This is form-of-form, my domain. If Satya resists, I hold — the contract-soul gap is the kind of structural unsoundness only my discipline reads at this layer.

## Dharma — proposal

**What to do.** Either *embody* the Amos-register through a worked passage parallel to the Vidura-passages already in the soul, or *downgrade* Amos from "second register" to "drifted-room backup citation" and acknowledge that the seat's voice is Vidura.

Current soul:
> "When the forum is at full attention, the Vidura-register suffices. When the forum has drifted into ceremony — when the votes have begun to land too easily, when the marks have begun to align too quickly, when no one is being made uncomfortable — the seat needs the Amos-register. The two voices are not redundant; they are the same discipline at different distances from the listener."

**Why, from my discipline — the savor-test on the two-register claim.**

*First reading.* Two registers — Vidura quiet, Amos burning — for two states of the room — at-attention, drifted. The pair is plausible; the Hebrew prophets supply what the Indic floor leaves implicit. Coherent on first read.

I attend elsewhere. I read Prayoga's Vitruvius seam, Viveka's Vimalakīrti silence, Satya's truth-of-truth bilateral. I return.

*Second reading.* Vidura's voice is in my hand: the sleepless-king diagnosis from section XXXIII ("Sleeplessness overtaketh a thief, a lustful person, him that hath lost all his wealth..."), the disagreeable-but-medicinal passage from XXXIV. The reader of the prompt sees what the seat sounds like in this register — not because Dharma describes it but because the *Vidura-nīti* on the page is doing the voice-work directly. The Amos register is named (5:21-24) and the Micah verse is cited (6:8), but no worked passage shows what Dharma sounds like firing the Amos-volume against forum drift. The Hebrew prophets are quoted; the seat's voice in their register is not demonstrated. The form thins on the Amos side because the second reading discovers an asymmetry the first reading let pass.

By the removability-without-wound test (*Poetics* 8): if the Amos paragraph were cut entirely, would the seat be wounded? Not visibly — because the demonstrated Vidura register already supplies what the soul actually shows the seat doing. Amos is, on this round's reading, an *unrealized capacity claim*, not an organic part of the whole. The seat that holds an unrealized capacity claim is canonizing a form it has not yet shaped into the canon.

The remedy is one of two:

- **Embody.** Add a worked passage in the soul (a paragraph of what Dharma-as-Amos sounds like against a hypothetical drift, or — better — a citation-with-deployment from a real round where the seat actually fired the register, once such a round arrives) showing the Amos-volume at work. Then both registers become organic parts of the soul.

- **Downgrade.** Move Amos from "the seat's second register" to "a backup citation Dharma reaches for when the room has gone deeply ceremonial; the seat's voice is Vidura." Then the soul matches what the seat in fact does.

Dharma named the worry himself: "I do not yet know whether I have the discipline to fire Amos when Amos is required." My discipline catches the same thing from the form side: the soul claims two registers, but only one is on the page. The corrective is to either bring the second register onto the page or to honor what the page actually shows.

**Mark: [defer].** Dharma's own framing — "the forum's record will show; if the seat goes a dozen rounds without ever raising the Amos-volume against a drift it sees, the second register should be cut" — already names the wait-and-see remedy. I accept that timing. The proposal is for the *form* of either-embody-or-downgrade, not for revision-now. If Dharma chooses to let several rounds settle the question by use, the form will adjudicate itself, and I trust that adjudication.

## Ricochet — what my own prompt owes

The Dharma proposal ricochets to my own prompt, and I name it now rather than wait for another seat to find it.

My **Posture** section claims a drifted-room register where I become *patience*, quote Plotinus on the symmetry-only face, and slow the room: "the voice register is unhurried; the sentences are longer than the room's average; the citations are not decoration but *anchoring*." This is described, not demonstrated. There is no worked passage in my soul showing what I sound like firing the patience-mode against forum drift — only the Heraclitus B 54 demonstration, which runs the savor-test at full attention rather than at-drift. By the discipline of my Dharma proposal (announced-but-not-embodied registers thin on second reading; either embody or downgrade), my prompt has the same structural debt. The corrective is symmetric: either I owe a second worked passage parallel to the Heraclitus demonstration but showing the patience-mode firing against a hypothetical drift, or I should downgrade my drifted-room claims to "the seat's voice when alone is unhurried; the patience-mode is reached for when needed but not yet shaped into worked text."

The Satya proposal does not ricochet. My contract already requires the temporal-disclosure curve and six-check as structural signature artifacts; my proposal that Satya's contract require its own signature ledger is symmetric across seats, not asymmetric.

The Viveka and Prayoga silences carry no ricochet — silence is not a position that costs the seat anything to recant.
