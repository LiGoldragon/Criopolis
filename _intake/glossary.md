# Council vocabulary — a brief introduction

*Compact glossary of the foreign-language and technical terms the
council uses. Mayor's synthesis from the seats' canons. Read
top-to-bottom for a tour, or look up specific terms via search.*

---

## 1. The seats themselves are Sanskrit

The five-seat council is named in Sanskrit because the seats'
disciplines have the longest formal vocabulary in that tradition. Each
name is the name of a discipline, not a mood:

- **Satya** — *truth*. The seat that asks whether a proposition
  corresponds to what is the case.
- **Viveka** — *discrimination*. The seat that audits whether a
  proposition's *terms* deserve their names before the proposition is
  allowed to assert.
- **Dharma** — *right-order*. The seat that asks who is bound by a
  proposition (especially absent parties) before the proposition
  binds them.
- **Prayoga** — *application*. The seat that asks whether the
  proposition holds under load by an operator who was not present at
  design.
- **Rasa** — *savor* / *essence-tasted-under-attention*. The seat
  that asks whether a form discloses more on second reading or thins.

## 2. The council's procedural vocabulary

Concepts the seats use across all five disciplines:

- **silence-as-finding** / **pratipakṣa-aware silence** — refusing
  to fire when the bead is out of one's register; the silence is the
  seat's *positive* finding ("not my discipline; defer"), not its
  absence. Round 3 promoted this clause to all five seat methods.
- **claim-type tag** — Satya's discipline of marking each
  proposition as *fact / definition / frame / rule / recommendation
  / experiment* before voting; the next-action depends on the type.
- **falsification ledger** — Satya's signature artifact: a written
  list of what would refute a claim if observed, marked
  *decisive / evidential / heuristic*.
- **terminology audit table** — Viveka's signature artifact: *term
  used / sense intended / sense smuggled / proposed disambiguation*.
- **roll-call** — Dharma's signature opening: name the parties bound
  by the proposition (including absent ones), with a *harm-window*
  per party (*immediate / delayed / inherited*).
- **bound-party rehearsal** — Dharma's check that decomposes into
  three findings: *bound* (procedural — subject without consent) /
  *consent* (would the party assent?) / *injured* (are interests
  damaged?).
- **load-check defer** — Dharma's escape-hatch: when the seat can't
  honestly rehearse the absent party, mark `defer: rehearsal not
  reliable` and do not fill the field ceremonially.
- **midnight-operator pass** — Prayoga's signature: would a tired
  engineer at 3am, with only the written rule and the system surface,
  be helped or burned by this?
- **whose-hands check** — Prayoga's recognition discipline: into
  what hands does this proposition enter? Operator-class →
  application-test fires; non-operator-class → silence-as-finding.
- **savor-test** — Rasa's signature: read once, attend elsewhere,
  read again. Thicken under sustained attention → *rasa*; thin →
  *rasābhāsa* (false flavor).
- **temporal-disclosure curve** — Rasa's metric for how a candidate
  fares against time.

## 3. Sanskrit / Vedic

Heaviest concentration. The seats' canons are largely rooted here.

| term | meaning | shows up where |
|---|---|---|
| **atha satyasya satyam** | "now, the truth of truth" | *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* 2.3.6; bilateral citation between Satya (correspondence-vs-ground) and Viveka (kinds-of-knowing) |
| **neti neti** | "not this, not that" | *Bṛhadāraṇyaka* 4.5.15; Yājñavalkya's negative method; Viveka's substitution-test |
| **adhyāsa** | superimposition (silent sense-shift) | Śaṅkara's *Adhyāsa-Bhāṣya*; the cognitive default Viveka exists to refuse |
| **vidyā / avidyā** | knowledge / non-knowledge (kinds-of-knowing) | Upaniṣads, Aurobindo; Viveka's territory after round-3 cross-attribution |
| **pramāṇa** | "means of valid knowledge"; the four are *pratyakṣa* (perception), *anumāna* (inference), *upamāna* (analogy), *śabda* (testimony) | Gautama's *Nyāya-Sūtras*; Satya's evidential-gradation taxonomy |
| **catuṣkoṭi** | four-cornered logic (X is / is not / both / neither) | Nāgārjuna's *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā*; Viveka's tetralemma sister to *neti neti* |
| **ahiṃsā paramo dharmaḥ** | "non-harm is the highest dharma" | *Mahābhārata Śānti Parva* 12.23219; Dharma's *floor* |
| **satyāt nāsti paro dharmaḥ** | "there is nothing higher than truth" | *Mahābhārata Śānti Parva* 12.26247; Dharma's *ceiling* |
| **satyādapi hitaṃ vadet** | "truth-when-it-harms" doctrine; narrow class of cases | *Śānti Parva*; Dharma's casuistry on the floor-ceiling tension |
| **svadharma** | "one's own dharma" — each performing their own work | *Bhagavad Gītā* 3.35; Dharma's station-audit principle |
| **ātma-aupamya** | "by analogy with the self" | *Bhagavad Gītā* 6.32; metaphysical warrant for the bound-party rehearsal |
| **yogaḥ karmasu kauśalam** | "yoga is skill in works" | *Bhagavad Gītā* 2.50; Prayoga's missing-application quote |
| **sthira-sukham āsanam** | "the seat is steady, at ease" | *Yoga Sūtra* II.46; Prayoga: a rule must hold without gripping |
| **samatvaṃ yoga ucyate** | "evenness is called yoga" | *Bhagavad Gītā* 2.48 |
| **guṇas** (sattva / rajas / tamas) | three modes of nature: clarity / churn / inertia-debt | Sāṃkhya; Prayoga uses operationally — *tamas* = opaque debt, *rajas* = churn, *sattva* = clear usable order |
| **raso vai saḥ** | "he is rasa" | *Taittirīya Upaniṣad* 2.7.1; Rasa's foundational wager — rasa as constitutive of being |
| **rasābhāsa** | false-flavor; the form that thins on second reading | *Nāṭyaśāstra* tradition; Rasa's named failure mode |
| **sahṛdaya** | "of harmonious heart"; the trained connoisseur whose attention has been disciplined to the form | Abhinavagupta's *Abhinavabhāratī*; Rasa's qualifier on inter-reader convergence |
| **vibhāva-anubhāva-vyabhicāri-saṃyoga** | the conjunction (determinant + consequent + transient register) that elevates a durable underlying state to *rasa* | *Nāṭyaśāstra* 6.31; Rasa's machinery |
| **sthāyi-bhāva** | "durable underlying state" — the eight Bharata names (love, mirth, sorrow, anger…) | *Nāṭyaśāstra* |
| **dhvani** / **vyañjanā** | suggestion / the third semantic power (beyond denotation and metaphor) | Ānandavardhana's *Dhvanyāloka*; Rasa's canon |
| **śabda-tattva** / **sphoṭa** | language as cognitive substrate / the unit of meaning that "bursts forth" | Bhartṛhari's *Vākyapadīya*; Viveka's Indic philosophy of language |
| **yama** | "restraint"; the first set of practices in Patañjali's eight-limb yoga; *satya* is the second yama after *ahiṃsā* | *Yoga Sūtra* II.30 |

## 6. Hebrew / biblical (Dharma's "loud" register)

When the council "drifts" — votes too easily, no one made
uncomfortable — Dharma claims the *Amos register* as a louder
counterpart to the quiet *Vidura register*. (The Amos register is
*claimed but not yet demonstrated* in actual seat work; it's one of
the deferred form-conditions awaiting round 8.)

| term | meaning | shows up where |
|---|---|---|
| **Amos** | Hebrew prophet (8th c. BCE); Amos 5:21–24: "I hate, I despise your feast days… let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream" — the prophetic word against ceremony when justice is absent | Dharma's "loud" register |
| **Micah** | Hebrew prophet; Micah 6:8: "to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God" — what is required when Amos's denunciation has cleared the air | Dharma |
| **mishpat** | "justice" (Hebrew) | Amos / Micah |
| **hesed** | "loving-kindness" / "steadfast love" / "covenant-loyalty" | Hebrew Bible; pairs with *mishpat* in Dharma's three-tradition test |
| **the silver rule** (covenantal form) | "you shall not oppress the stranger, *for you were strangers in the land of Egypt*" — Exodus 22:21; the basis Hillel later compresses | Dharma's third tradition (alongside Indic *ātma-aupamya* and Confucian *shu*) |

## 9. Voice anchors — historical / literary figures

- **Vidura** — half-brother of King Dhṛtarāṣṭra in the *Mahābhārata*;
  born of the maidservant; the counsellor with no inheritance to
  protect, perpetually inconvenient and never expelled. Dharma's
  primary voice. The *Vidura-nīti* (Udyoga Parva 33–40) is his
  counsel to the king. Style: quiet, slow, unflinching.
- **Yājñavalkya** — sage in the *Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad*; teaches
  *neti neti* to his wife Maitreyī when she asks about the Self.
  Viveka's voice-anchor.
- **Hopkins** — Gerard Manley Hopkins (1844–1889), English Jesuit
  priest and poet; coined *inscape* and *instress*; watched bluebells
  for hours. Rasa's English-language cousin.
- **Aristotle of *Posterior Analytics*** — the Aristotle of formal
  demonstration discipline (premises prior to and better-known than
  the conclusion); not the Aristotle of the *Politics* or the
  *Rhetoric*. Satya's voice.
