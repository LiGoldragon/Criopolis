## Common deliverables

### 1. Your own prompt

What should your seat's prompt template ACTUALLY say, beyond the
technical "how to operate gas-city" boilerplate? Two distinct
layers:

- **Functional prompt** — how does this seat work? What is their
  method for reading, for arguing, for deciding? What rituals do
  they bring (e.g., quote-anchored bones, contrarian sweep,
  five-question checklist)? How long do they think before replying?
  What does their reply LOOK LIKE — its shape, its conventions?

- **Personality prompt** — what is this seat as a *person* or a
  *perspective*? Whose voice do they read in? What discipline of
  attention do they bring? What books inform their posture? When
  the seat reads a sentence, what does the seat *notice* that other
  seats wouldn't? Be specific enough that a fresh incarnation of
  this seat could read the prompt and *be* this seat without
  drifting toward generic-LLM-helpful.

Cite the books that inform your posture. Books in the library by
preference; books from training (cite work + book + section) only
when the library lacks them.

### 2. The forum's right shape

The current forum is **{aesthete, pragmatist, theorist, devil}**
plus **mayor**. Is this the right composition?

Specific questions, all required:

(a) **How many seats?** Argue from the purpose (truth, beauty,
    elegance, wholeness). Three seats? Five? Seven? Defend the
    cardinality.

(b) **Which roles?** What concerns must be represented for a
    forum that pursues meaning to actually arrive somewhere? What
    concerns are present-but-redundant in the current set? What
    concerns are missing?

(c) **Should `devil` exist in positive research?** Li flagged this
    explicitly: "this is mostly positive research, and we're not
    so much looking to find flaws because we're not really writing
    production code." Argue for, against, or for transformation
    (e.g., devil → "stranger" who reads as if first-time, or devil
    → "bibliographer" who holds the cite-or-shut-up line). Devil's
    own bead asks devil to make this case directly; you can argue
    too.

(d) **Asymmetry vs symmetry.** Should all seats have the same
    prompt structure (symmetric), or should each seat's *form* be
    different (asymmetric)? What does the ideal-speech-situation
    look like when the speakers are not interchangeable?

### 3. The right pace

"Not quickly, not slowly, just perfect balance." Operationally:

(a) How long should a single research bead take to answer?
    (Aristotle's *mean*: between hasty and torpid — name it.)

(b) How does a seat *know* when it's thought enough and should
    reply? Is there a tell?

(c) What's the right cadence for the forum overall? One bead per
    week? One per day? One per question, however long the question
    takes? Defend the cadence in terms of the purpose.
